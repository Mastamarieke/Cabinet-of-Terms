***Executives speak in public. Sometimes, in doing so, they reveal more than they intended.***

This cluster treats quoted statements as analytical objects — sentences that expose the categorical frameworks, strategic calculations, and institutional assumptions of the people who made them. Not what was said, but what the saying reveals.

The question this cluster asks: what does this sentence assume about the world?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [["Like Prime, but with human beings."]]
Caleb Vitello, ICE, on ImmigrationOS. Package logistics applied to deportation. Said without noticing it was said.

### [["Adding amplitude to war is obviously an issue."]]
Brian McClendon, Niantic, Bellingfest 2024. An acknowledgement that is not a refusal. The word "issue" doing all the work.

### [["We have never and will never sell user data."]]
Mark Zuckerberg, Meta. Technically true. Designed to be understood as meaning something it does not say.

### [["During a dynamic political environment."]]
Internal Meta memo on launch timing. "Dynamic" means "distracted." Launch when attention is elsewhere.

### [["500 million people installed that app in 60 days."]]
Brian McClendon, Niantic, on Pokémon Go. Scale as self-evident value. Five hundred million unknowing data contributors.

### [["I could definitely see it."]]
Brian McClendon, Niantic, asked whether militaries could buy the Large Geospatial Model. Neither yes nor no. Every door left open.

### [[Tu Nube Seca Mi Río]]
Spanish activist collective. Your cloud dries my river. Four words that name what a data centre does to a watershed.

### [[Adolescence (BBC, 2025)]]
A television drama as analytical object. Cultural evidence that a phenomenon — manosphere radicalisation — has reached the mainstream.
