# "Adding amplitude to war is obviously an issue."


***An acknowledgement that is not a refusal. The word "issue" doing all the work.***

**The statement:** *"Adding amplitude to war is obviously an issue."* — **Brian McClendon**, SVP Engineering at **Niantic**, speaking at Bellingfest 2024, when asked about the military implications of **Niantic**'s Large Geospatial Model.

**The context:** **Niantic**'s Large Geospatial Model (LGM) is a foundational AI model trained on geospatial data — including data collected by Pokémon Go players over a decade of gameplay. At Bellingfest 2024, a conference on open-source intelligence and digital investigation, McClendon was asked whether the LGM's capabilities — precise real-world spatial mapping — could be used to make military operations more effective. This was his response.

**What the sentence does:** The sentence acknowledges a harm ("adding amplitude to war") and then performs concern ("obviously an issue") without committing to any response. The structure is: *X is bad* + *I have noticed that X is bad* = implicit claim to ethical awareness, without ethical constraint. "Issue" is the key word: it describes a problem to be managed, not a line to be held. Compare: "adding amplitude to war is therefore a line we will not cross." That sentence was not said.

> An acknowledgement of potential harm that substitutes awareness for refusal — where naming something as an issue closes the conversation rather than opening it.

**The analytical work the sentence does:** It is a textbook instance of what might be called ethical hedging: the speaker demonstrates awareness of a moral problem, signals that they take it seriously, and says nothing that would constrain future action. [[Dual-Use Technology]] — technology with both civilian and military applications — is the structural category. The sentence acknowledges the dual-use problem without resolving it. [[Military-Industrial Complex]] — the entanglement of technology companies and defence — is the context in which the hedge is intelligible: the door is left open because the commercial relationship has value.

**Why this sentence matters:** Once you hear the structure — "X is obviously an issue" — you can identify it wherever it appears. It is the rhetorical form of corporate ethical consciousness that produces no constraint. The acknowledgement is the performance. The refusal is what was not said.
