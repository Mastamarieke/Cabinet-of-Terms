# "During a dynamic political environment."


***"Dynamic" means distracted. Launch when attention is elsewhere.***

**The statement:** *"During a dynamic political environment."* — from an internal Meta memo discussing the launch timing of the Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses' Name Tag feature — facial recognition capability that identifies people by name from a photograph — reported by 404 Media in 2024.

**The context:** The Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses include a camera and AI features. The Name Tag feature — which can identify a person's name from their face — raised immediate privacy concerns when it became known. Internal Meta communications discussed launch timing in relation to the political environment, using this phrase to describe the period around the US 2024 election cycle.

**What the sentence does:** "Internal Meta documents described the rollout of facial-recognition-enabled smart glasses as taking place within a “dynamic political environment,” a phrase critics interpreted as corporate language for moments when public attention, regulation, and civil-society scrutiny are fragmented by larger crises. Rather than directly framing **biometric surveillance** as an ethical or democratic issue, the language reframes it as a matter of timing, communication strategy, and reputational risk management.

The controversy reflects broader concerns about ambient surveillance: wearable AI systems that continuously observe, identify, and analyze people in public space. Critics argue that smart glasses equipped with facial recognition normalize passive biometric tracking in everyday life, eroding meaningful consent because bystanders cannot realistically opt out of being scanned, identified, or datafied.

**Why this sentence matters:** "Dynamic political environment" is a useful phrase to recognise across contexts.
