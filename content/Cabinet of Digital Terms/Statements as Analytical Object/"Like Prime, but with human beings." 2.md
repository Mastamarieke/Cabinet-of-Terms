# "Like Prime, but with human beings."


***Logistics language applied to deportation. Said without noticing it was said.***

**The statement:** *"Like Prime, but with human beings."* — Caleb Vitello, Acting Director of US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), describing ImmigrationOS — **Palantir**'s deportation case management platform — to journalists, 2025.

**The context:** ImmigrationOS is a **Palantir**-built software system contracted by ICE for managing immigration enforcement operations: tracking individuals, scheduling detentions, coordinating deportation logistics. Vitello offered the Amazon Prime comparison as an explanation of the system's efficiency and comprehensiveness.

**What the sentence assumes:** Amazon Prime optimises the movement of packages from warehouse to delivery point. The optimisation is for speed, accuracy, and scale. The comparison assumes that the same optimisation logic — applied to people — is self-evidently positive: more efficient, more comprehensive, better. The sentence treats the persons being deported as the packages being delivered. The dehumanisation is not polemical — it is operational. The categorical framework was so embedded that it was offered as an explanation, not a boast.

> Logistics vocabulary applied to deportation — not as a rhetorical choice, but as an operational framework that reveals how the speaker understands the people being processed.

**The analytical work the sentence does:** It makes visible what [[Dehumanization]] — Bandura's moral disengagement — looks like when it is not extreme but mundane: not "these people are **subhuman**" but "these people are inventory." The bureaucratic register is the mechanism. [[Algorithmic Violence]] — harm produced by automated systems without individual decisions — is the system ImmigrationOS operationalises. [[Surveillance Capitalism]] — human behaviour as raw material — is the commercial context: **Palantir** builds the infrastructure; ICE deploys it; the language of package logistics names the logic.

**Why this sentence matters:** Once you hear "like Prime, but with human beings," every logistics metaphor applied to people becomes audible as a category choice. The language is not neutral. It encodes an institutional framework. The sentence revealed it.
