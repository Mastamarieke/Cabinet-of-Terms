# Bot Farms


***The opinion was trending. Thousands of accounts were sharing it. Most of those accounts were created last month.***

**Literal meaning:** **Bot farms** are networks of automated or semi-automated social media accounts — operated at scale from centralised infrastructure — used to manipulate the apparent popularity of content, amplify specific messages, harass individuals, or create the impression of grassroots support for positions that are actually coordinated campaigns.

**Origin:** The concept emerged with the scaling of social media from the early 2010s. Oxford Internet Institute's Computational Propaganda Project documented **bot farms** across 70 countries by 2019, finding them used by governments, political campaigns, and commercial operators. The Internet Research Agency — the Russian state-linked operation that interfered in the 2016 US election — became the most publicly documented example. Researchers including Samuel Woolley and Philip Howard produced the foundational academic documentation.

> Networks of automated fake accounts used to make fringe opinions look mainstream — manufacturing the appearance of public consensus where none exists.

**The Appeal:** From the operator's perspective, **bot farms** are efficient tools for influence at scale. For authoritarian governments, they suppress dissent and amplify state narratives. For commercial operators, they inflate follower counts and engagement metrics. The cost per interaction is extremely low.

**The Friction:** [[Recommender Systems]] — algorithms that determine what you see — amplify based on engagement signals. **Bot farms** manufacture those signals, feeding the algorithm content it then distributes to real users. The manipulation is structural: the platform cannot easily distinguish manufactured engagement from genuine engagement. [[Flood the Zone]] — information overload as strategic destabilisation — is enabled by **bot farms**: automated accounts can produce more noise than human moderators can process. [[Microtargeting]] uses real user data; **bot farms** manufacture fake user signals that distort the data environment both systems depend on.

**Why This Matters:** **Bot farms** make visible that social proof — the signal that tells you what others think — can be manufactured at scale. Once you know that, "trending" and "viral" become different kinds of sentences.
