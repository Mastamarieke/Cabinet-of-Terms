# Algospeak


***People write "unalive" instead of "die" — not to be clever, but because the algorithm removes the real words.***

**Literal meaning:** **Algospeak** is the language people use on social media to slip past automated content filters. It is a form of self-censorship where users invent coded words, altered spellings, emojis, and euphemisms to keep their posts from being flagged or removed by algorithmic moderation systems.

**Origin:** The term _algospeak_ gained wider public and academic attention around 2022, particularly following reporting by Taylor Lorenz in _The Washington Post_ and subsequent research on algorithmic content moderation and platform censorship.Common examples include "unalive" for suicide or death, "SA" for sexual assault, "corn" for porn, "seggs" for sex, and "le dollar bean" for the clitoris. Research by Karahalios and colleagues at the University of Illinois documented how content moderation shapes language through the reactions it provokes, creating parallel vocabularies that evolve as moderation systems adapt.

> Language actively shaped by content moderation — where the vocabulary in use on a platform reflects the boundaries the algorithm enforces, not just what users want to say.

**The Appeal:** **Algospeak** enables communities to maintain conversations about important topics — mental health, sexuality, health information, political situations — in environments where direct language is penalised. For marginalised communities in particular, **algospeak** preserves access to platforms they depend on. "Unalive" allows discussion of suicidality without triggering suppression of crisis support content.

**The Friction:** The language deformation has costs. Mental health crisis content, harm reduction information, and support communities are forced into euphemism — making them harder to find for people who need them and harder to study for researchers. [[Recommender Systems]] are not neutral moderators: their suppression patterns reflect the interests and blindspots of the platforms that design them. [[Deplatforming]] of communities produces **algospeak** as a direct consequence: the community adapts its language to survive. The evolution is arms-race dynamic — as detection improves, substitutions proliferate.

**Why This Matters:** **Algospeak** makes the algorithm's moderation logic visible in the language itself. The words people use reveal what the platform has decided to suppress. Once you know that, the vocabulary is a map of the platform's priorities.
