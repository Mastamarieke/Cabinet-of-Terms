# BUMMER


***Jaron Lanier spent years inside Silicon Valley before writing down what it actually is: a machine for modifying human behaviour and renting the results.***

**Literal meaning:** **BUMMER** — Behaviours of Users Modified and Made into an Empire for Rent — is **Jaron Lanier**'s acronym for the dominant architecture of commercial social media platforms, introduced in *Ten Arguments for Deleting Your Social Media Accounts Right Now* (2018). It names the system by which platforms modify user behaviour through algorithmic manipulation and rent out the resulting behavioural influence to advertisers and political operators.

**Origin:** **Jaron Lanier**, one of the pioneers of virtual reality and a long-time Silicon Valley critic, introduced the acronym to provide a single handle for a complex system. The six letters unpack as: Behaviours of Users — the subject being shaped; Modified — the activity the system performs on users; Made into an Empire — the scale and power accrued; for Rent — the commercial model, selling access to the modification capacity.

> **Jaron Lanier**'s name for the social media business model: modify user behaviour at scale, then rent that influence to anyone who pays.

**The Appeal:** **BUMMER** names the system rather than criticising individual platforms — which makes it analytically more durable than platform-specific critiques. Lanier's argument is that the problem is not this or that company's decisions but the fundamental business model: advertising-funded behaviour modification.

**The Friction:** **BUMMER** as a concept names what [[Surveillance Capitalism]] (**Zuboff**) and [[Attention Economy]] (Wu) describe from different angles: the same system of capturing human behaviour as raw material and renting the resulting influence. [[Recommender Systems]] are the technical implementation. [[Dopamine Feedback Loops]] are the psychological mechanism. [[Microtargeting]] is the rental product. Lanier's distinctive contribution is the "empire" framing: **BUMMER** has created a new kind of power — not a state, not a corporation in the traditional sense, but a rented modification capacity that operates at civilisational scale.

**Why This Matters:** **BUMMER** gives a single name to a distributed system. Once you have the name, the system is visible as a system — not as a series of individual platform decisions, but as an architecture with consistent properties across all its implementations.
