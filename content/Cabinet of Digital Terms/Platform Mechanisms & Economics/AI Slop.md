***You read the article to the end and retained nothing. Not because you were distracted — because there was nothing there.***

**Literal meaning:** **AI slop** is low-quality, mass-produced AI-generated content — articles, images, social media posts, product descriptions — that floods information environments with material that is superficially plausible but lacks genuine thought, expertise, or purpose. The term was popularised by developer and writer Simon Willison in 2024.

**Origin:** Willison coined "**AI slop**" in May 2024 as a term for AI-generated content that is identifiably low-effort — the digital equivalent of factory-farmed food. The term spread quickly because it named something people were already experiencing: search results cluttered with AI-generated articles, social media feeds filled with AI-generated images, email inboxes receiving AI-generated spam.

> Mass AI-generated content that fills the web with material that looks like information but contains no thought, expertise, or purpose.

**The Appeal:** **AI slop** is cheap to produce. For content farms, SEO operations, and automated marketing, the cost-per-word of AI-generated content approaches zero. The scale is unprecedented: a single operator can produce thousands of articles per day. For platforms that reward volume and engagement over quality, slop is economically rational.

**The Friction:** [[Synthetic Generation]] — AI-generated content at scale — is the mechanism; **AI slop** is the environmental outcome. [[Recommender Systems]] — algorithms that determine what you see — are trained partly on engagement signals that slop can fake: clicks, dwell time, shares. [[Age of Average]] — the algorithmic homogenisation of content — is what slop accelerates: when AI generates to the median of training data, everything converges toward the same bland centre. [[Slopification]] is the systemic term for the process; **AI slop** is the individual instance. Search engines, journalism, and education are all affected.

**Why This Matters:** **AI slop** is not a temporary problem. It is the predictable outcome of making content production free and fast. Once you know the term, the question to ask of any piece of content is not just *is this true* but *was this made by someone who thought about it*.
