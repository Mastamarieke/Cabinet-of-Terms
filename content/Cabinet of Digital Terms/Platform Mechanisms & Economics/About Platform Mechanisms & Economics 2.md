# Platform Mechanisms & Economics


***The platform is not a neutral tool. It is a designed environment that shapes behaviour in the interest of the people who built it.***

This cluster maps how platforms operate, steer, and profit. The terms here describe the technical and economic architecture that produces user behaviour — not as an accident but as an outcome. Recommender systems surface content that keeps you engaged. Dark patterns make it harder to leave than to stay. Gamification creep turns every interaction into a scored performance.

The vocabulary here is useful because it names things that are easier to resist once named. You cannot unsubscribe from the attention economy, but you can recognise when it is working on you.

The question this cluster asks: what is this platform designed to make you do?

## Terms in this cluster

### [[AI as a Utility]]
AI as infrastructure — like electricity or water. Useful, invisible, and dependent on systems you do not control.

### [[AI Slop]]
Mass AI-generated content of low quality, filling the web. The floor of the information environment falling away.

### [[Attention Economy]]
Attention as scarce raw material. Every platform is competing for the same limited supply of human focus.

### [[Attention Harvesting]]
The systematic extraction of attention through interface design. Not a side effect — the primary function.

### [[Biometric Surveillance]]
Body signals — heartrate, face, gait — as data points. The body becomes legible to the platform.

### [[Bot Farms]]
Networks of automated accounts that simulate human presence to manipulate public discourse at scale.

### [[BUMMER]]
Lanier's acronym: Behaviours of Users Modified and Made into an Empire for Rent. The business model, spelt out.

### [[Clickbait]]
Content designed to generate clicks, not to inform. The headline is the product.

### [[Dark Patterns]]
Brignull, 2010. Interface design that misleads users — making the wrong choice easier than the right one. Now codified in EU law.

### [[Deceptive Design]]
The legally and academically preferred term for dark patterns. Stronger in regulatory and judicial contexts.

### [[Dopamine Feedback Loops]]
Variable reward schedules that keep users active. The slot machine in your pocket.

### [[Empathy Exploit]]
Empathy as a design strategy — emotional connection engineered to bind or manipulate users.

### [[Enshittification]]
Doctorow: platforms structurally deteriorate for users over time. First they attract users, then advertisers, then they extract from both.

### [[Gamification Creep]]
Game logic entering non-game contexts. Points, streaks, and leaderboards in places that were never games.

### [[Ludic Loop]]
Schüll: the cycle of anticipation, action, and reward that sustains compulsive engagement. Designed in, not designed out. *(also in: Consequences of Digital Behaviour)* *(new V10)*

### [[Microtargeting]]
Highly specific audience targeting based on behavioural data. The same message, tuned differently for each recipient.

### [[Neuromarketing]]
Measuring brain activity to optimise persuasion. The consumer research that does not ask — it observes.

### [[Nudging]]
Steering behaviour through environmental design, without prohibition or instruction. The default as the decision.

### [[Predatory Design]]
Design choices aimed at maximum playtime and spending, particularly targeting children.

### [[Ragebaiting]]
Content deliberately designed to provoke outrage. Anger is the most shareable emotion.

### [[Recommender Systems]]
Algorithms that determine what you see. Not a window on the world — an editorial system with hidden criteria.

### [[Shadow Banning]]
Invisibly restricting a user's reach without notifying them. Moderation exercised without accountability or appeal. *(new V10)*

### [[Synthetic Generation]]
AI-generated content at scale. Text, images, video — produced faster than humans can evaluate it.
