# Ludic Loop

***You opened the app to check one thing. Forty minutes later you are still there.***

**Literal meaning**
*Ludic* — from Latin *ludus*: play. *Loop* — a repeating cycle with no defined exit. A **Ludic Loop** is a design pattern that uses the cycle of anticipation, action, and reward to sustain compulsive engagement.

**Origin**
Natasha Dow Schüll coined the concept in *Addiction by Design* (2012), documenting how electronic slot machines are engineered to produce a trance-like state she calls "the machine zone" — where players seek not to win but to maintain the loop. The concept was rapidly applied to mobile games, social media feeds, and notification systems, which share the same structural logic.

> A designed cycle of anticipation, action, and reward with no natural endpoint — keeping you engaged not through satisfaction but through the promise of it.

**The Appeal**
The loop produces genuine pleasure: the rhythm of action and reward is satisfying. Games, feeds, and notification systems that use ludic loops are often experienced as enjoyable, not coercive.

**The Friction**
The loop is designed to have no natural endpoint. [[Dopamine Feedback Loops]] are the neurological mechanism; the **ludic loop** is the design expression. Schüll documents that casino designers explicitly optimise for time-on-device rather than winning — the same metric that governs social media platform design. The pleasure is real; the endpoint is absent by design.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see the **ludic loop** in your phone's notification system, your social media feed, and your inbox, you see that the experience of compulsion is not a weakness — it is a specification.
