# Clickbait


***You clicked because you had to know. The article told you nothing.***

**Literal meaning:** **Clickbait** describes headlines, thumbnails, and titles deliberately designed to provoke curiosity or emotional response sufficient to generate a click — without the content behind the click necessarily delivering what was implied. The bait is the headline; the click is the transaction.

**Origin:** The term emerged in online journalism and content marketing from around 2012–2014, as social media sharing made headlines the primary determinant of traffic. BuzzFeed's early editorial model — highly shareable, curiosity-gap headlines — became the dominant template. Academic research by Chakraborty et al. (2016) at IW3C2 produced the first large-scale analysis of **clickbait** patterns, identifying rhetorical structures: forward-referencing ("you won't believe"), number lists ("7 things"), and emotional triggers.

> Content designed to generate clicks rather than inform — where the headline promises more than the article delivers, because the click is the product.

**The Appeal:** **Clickbait** works. Curiosity-gap headlines demonstrably outperform descriptive headlines in click-through rates. For publishers competing for social media traffic in an **attention economy**, the technique is economically rational. Some **clickbait** content is also genuinely good — the headline gets people to read a story they would not have found otherwise.

**The Friction:** The optimisation target is the click, not the read, the understanding, or the satisfaction. [[Recommender Systems]] amplify **clickbait** because clicks are the signal they are trained on — regardless of whether the click produced value. [[Ragebaiting]] is the emotional intensification: not just curiosity but outrage as the trigger. [[Empathy Exploit]] is the softer variant: emotional hooks around suffering or triumph. [[AI Slop]] accelerates the dynamic: when content production is free, the only variable that matters is the headline — and AI-generated **clickbait** at scale floods the information environment.

**Why This Matters:** **Clickbait** makes visible that the headline is a separate product from the article — engineered for a different metric. Once you know that, the gap between what was promised and what was delivered is not a mistake. It is the design.
