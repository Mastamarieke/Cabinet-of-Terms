# Toxic Positivity


***She lost her job. The comments said: everything happens for a reason.***

**Literal meaning:** **Toxic positivity** describes the cultural pressure to maintain a positive outlook regardless of circumstances — dismissing, minimising, or reframing negative emotions as obstacles to wellbeing rather than valid responses to experience. The "toxic" qualifier distinguishes it from genuine optimism: it names positivity as a compulsory performance rather than an authentic state.

**Origin:** The term developed in psychology and counselling practice as a description of a communication pattern that invalidates emotional experience. It entered popular discourse in the mid-2010s, gaining significant traction on social media from around 2020, when wellness culture and the pressures of the COVID-19 pandemic made compulsory positivity both more prevalent and more contested.

> The compulsory performance of optimism — where authentic negative emotion is treated as a failure of attitude rather than a reasonable response.

**The Appeal:** Optimism and resilience are genuinely beneficial. The impulse to reframe difficulty as opportunity comes from a real desire to help — and for many people, in many circumstances, positive reframing does help. The wellness industry that distributes **toxic positivity** also distributes genuine coping tools.

**The Friction:** The compulsion is the problem. [[That Girl]] — the lifestyle aesthetic that performs wellness as identity — runs on **toxic positivity**: the difficult morning is the discipline, the failure is the data, the setback is the character-building. [[Highlight Reel]] content makes this ambient: when everyone's visible narrative is one of growth and gratitude, negative emotion becomes invisible and therefore shameful. The person grieving, struggling, or simply exhausted is surrounded by content that implicitly codes their experience as insufficient. Psychologist Susan David describes this as "emotional bypassing": the positive language bypasses the emotion rather than processing it.

**Why This Matters:** **Toxic positivity** makes visible the difference between genuine wellbeing and its performance. Once you know the term, "everything happens for a reason" lands differently — as a social norm, not a comfort.
