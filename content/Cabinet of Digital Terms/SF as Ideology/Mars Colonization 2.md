# Mars Colonization


***Escape from Earth, privately funded, personally led. The philanthropy of leaving.***

> Escape from Earth as a privately funded, personally led project — where the language of species survival conceals the politics of who gets to leave.

**Literal meaning:** **Mars colonization** describes the aspiration to establish a self-sustaining human settlement on Mars — SpaceX's stated long-term goal under **Elon Musk**'s direction. The project involves developing launch and transit vehicles, life support infrastructure, and ultimately the conditions for a multi-planetary human civilisation.

**Origin:** The aspiration to colonise Mars has deep roots in SF — from H.G. Wells to Ray Bradbury to Kim Stanley Robinson's *Mars* trilogy. As a contemporary policy-level project, it is associated primarily with **Elon Musk**, who has stated that making humanity multi-planetary is the primary purpose of SpaceX. Musk regularly provides updated timelines for crewed Mars missions. The timelines have consistently moved.

>In contemporary techno-utopian discourse, Mars colonisation functions not only as a scientific or engineering project, but also as a science-fiction narrative about civilisational continuity, technological destiny, and post-Earth futures — particularly within Silicon Valley, SpaceX culture, and longtermist ideology.

**The Appeal:** **Mars colonization** addresses the real problem of long-term civilisational risk: a single-planet species is vulnerable to planetary catastrophe. The backup civilisation argument is logically coherent. The engineering challenge is genuinely interesting. The SF legacy gives it cultural resonance and recruits talented people.

**The Friction:** The "multi-planetary species" framing obscures the ownership structure. SpaceX is a private company. The infrastructure for Mars would be owned by its investors. [[Fortress Futurism]] — technology as private refuge — is the relevant category: escape from a damaged Earth as a private project funded by those who can afford it. [[Longtermism]] provides the moral framework: the vast importance of ensuring humanity's long-term survival justifies extraordinary present investment in the exit infrastructure. [[Objectivism (Rand)]] is the philosophical substrate: the exceptional individual's project of civilisational preservation. The vocabulary of "we" — "we must become a multi-planetary species" — conceals that the "we" is funded by specific investors and controlled by specific individuals.

**Why This Matters:** **Mars colonization** makes visible how SF's species-survival vocabulary can be deployed to frame private commercial projects as universal human necessities. Once you ask who owns the escape route, the universalist framing becomes legible as something more specific.
