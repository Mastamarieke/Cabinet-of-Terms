***Death is an engineering problem. Aging is a bug. The body is legacy hardware.***

**Literal meaning:** **Transhumanism** is an intellectual and cultural movement that advocates for the use of technology to enhance human physical, cognitive, and psychological capacities — and ultimately to transcend current human limitations including ageing and death. It treats biological constraints as problems to be solved by engineering rather than conditions to be accepted.

**Origin:** The term was coined by Julian Huxley in a 1957 essay: "the human species can, if it wishes, transcend itself." The movement developed through the 1980s–90s in academic and SF communities, with figures including FM-2030 (Fereidoun M. Esfandiary), Max More, and Nick Bostrom providing philosophical frameworks. The World Transhumanist Association (now Humanity+) was founded in 1998. It became influential in Silicon Valley through its connections with the Extropy Institute and through its adoption by prominent technologists.

> An ideology that frames biological human constraints as engineering problems — where death is a bug and the body is legacy hardware.

**The Appeal:** **Transhumanism** addresses universal human concerns: suffering, illness, cognitive limitation, mortality. The aspiration to reduce suffering through technology is not radical — it is continuous with medicine. The extension of that aspiration to its limit is logically coherent, if speculative.

**The Friction:** **Transhumanism** as ideology does specific political work. It frames existing human social arrangements — with their inequalities, injustices, and power asymmetries — as contingent biological limitations to be overcome, rather than political conditions to be changed. [[Objectivism (Rand)]] provides the philosophical substrate: the exceptional individual transcending the merely human. [[Mind Uploading]] and [[Neuralink]] are the specific technical projects. [[Longtermism]] is **transhumanism**'s political cousin: both orient moral concern toward a post-human future while treating present human conditions as comparatively insignificant. [[Fortress Futurism]] is the survivalist variant: enhancement as preparation for catastrophe.

**Why This Matters:** **Transhumanism** names the ideology that makes Silicon Valley billionaires' personal immortality projects feel like universal human progress. Once you see who the "we" is in "we can transcend human limitations," the universalism of the claim becomes legible as a particular class interest.
