***Human-machine fusion as collective project. The collective framing obscures who owns the infrastructure.***

**Literal meaning:** **Cybernetic collective** describes the vision of a future society in which human cognition is networked with AI systems and other human minds — creating a collective intelligence that transcends individual limitation. The concept draws on cybernetics (Norbert Wiener's science of communication and control in systems) and on SF depictions of hive mind or networked consciousness.

**Origin:** The term is associated with **Elon Musk**'s public statements about **Neuralink**'s long-term vision: not merely brain-computer interfaces for medical purposes but a substrate for human cognitive enhancement and eventually collective intelligence. Musk has described the goal as enabling humans to keep pace with AI by merging with it. The **cybernetic collective** framing positions what would be a proprietary commercial technology as a species-level capability.

> A vision of networked human-AI consciousness framed as collective enhancement — where the infrastructure enabling it belongs to a small number of private actors.

**The Appeal:** The **cybernetic collective** addresses a real concern: if AI systems become more capable than individual humans across all domains, what is the role of human intelligence? The fusion model — augmenting rather than replacing — is one coherent answer. The collective framing has appeal: not just individual enhancement but shared intelligence.

**The Friction:** The vocabulary of "collective" obscures the ownership question. [[Neuralink]] is a private company. The infrastructure for any **cybernetic collective** would be owned by its investors and operated under their terms. [[Cyborg Musk]] is the personal brand expression of the same vision. [[Technofeudalism]] — platform owners as feudal lords — describes what collective infrastructure under private control actually produces. [[Cyberfeminism]] has engaged critically with **cyborg** collectives since **Haraway**: the question is always who designs the collective and whose values it embodies. A **cybernetic collective** built on Neuralink's infrastructure would be a Neuralink product, with Neuralink's terms of service.

**Why This Matters:** **Cybernetic collective** names the sleight of hand by which private technology projects are framed as collective human advancement. Once you identify the ownership structure behind the "we," the collective vocabulary becomes legible as branding.
