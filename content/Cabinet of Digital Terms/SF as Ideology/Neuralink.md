# Neuralink


***Brain-computer interface. The name sounds like science fiction because it was designed to.***

**Literal meaning:** **Neuralink** is a neurotechnology company founded by **Elon Musk** in 2016, developing implantable brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) — devices surgically inserted into the brain that read neural signals and translate them into digital commands. The company's first human implant was conducted in January 2024.

**Origin:** **Brain-computer interface** research has a documented scientific history stretching back to the 1960s, with academic work at BrainGate and other institutions producing clinically validated systems for paralysed patients before **Neuralink** existed. **Neuralink**'s contribution has been to commercialise and publicly amplify the technology. The name — neural + link — is itself SF aesthetic: clean, technical, combining biology and connectivity in a way that recalls cyberpunk fiction. Musk's stated long-term vision extends well beyond medical applications to cognitive enhancement and the **cybernetic collective**.

> A **brain-computer interface** company whose branding draws from science fiction rather than clinical practice — the aesthetic doing ideological work.

**The Appeal:** The legitimate medical applications of brain-computer interfaces are real and significant: restoring motor function to paralysed patients, treating neurological conditions, and enabling communication for those who have lost it. **Neuralink**'s first patient — a paralysed man who was able to control a computer cursor with his thoughts — is a genuine medical milestone.

**The Friction:** The brand does work that the technology does not. "**Neuralink**" implies neural networking at scale; the current device is a medical implant for paralysed patients. [[Brain-Computer Interface]] is the clinical term for the same technology, without the SF aesthetic. [[Cybernetic Collective]] and [[Mind Uploading]] are the long-term aspirations the brand implicitly promises. [[Transhumanism]] is the ideology the name signals membership in. The regulatory and ethical framework for implantable neurotechnology — informed consent, data ownership of neural signals, long-term safety, equitable access — is largely undeveloped. [[VSD (Value Sensitive Design)]] applied to neural interfaces raises questions that the brand vocabulary forecloses: whose values are encoded in the interface?

**Why This Matters:** **Neuralink** makes visible how a brand name can serve as ideological positioning — recruiting the cultural capital of SF to a commercial technology project before the technology delivers on what the name implies.
