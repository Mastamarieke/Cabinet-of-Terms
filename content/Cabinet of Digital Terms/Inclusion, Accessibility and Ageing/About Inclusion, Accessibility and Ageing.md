***The app was updated. The button moved. Nobody asked the people who needed it most.***

This cluster maps a blind spot in the digital debate. Digital systems structurally exclude certain groups — and that exclusion is a design choice, not an accident.

The question this cluster asks: who was not in the room when this was built?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [[Accessibility]]
What the term claims — and who is still excluded despite it.

### [[Universal Design]]
Design for everyone as the starting point. Not an add-on. Not a compliance checkbox.

### [[Inclusive Design]]
Designing from the margin improves the product for everyone. The exception reveals the rule.

### [[Digital Divide]]
Unequal access to digital infrastructure. Structural, not individual. The gap is not closing.

### [[Digital Exclusion]]
People actively excluded by design or policy. Not left behind — pushed out.

### [[Digital Ageing]]
Growing older in a digital society is not optional. The interface does not slow down with you.

### [[Silver Surfer]]
A positive label for older people online. Also patronising. Both things at once.

### [[Ageism (Digital)]]
Age discrimination in interface design, policy, and language. The assumption that older users do not count.

### [[Tech Abandonment]]
Older people giving up technology because it became too complex. A choice that was not really a choice.

### [[Technostress]]
Stress caused by using or dealing with technology. Documented across age groups. Rarely treated as a design failure.
