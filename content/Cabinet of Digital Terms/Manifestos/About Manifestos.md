# Manifestos


***These texts shaped how we think about technology, power, and the body. Their vocabulary still circulates — sometimes as inspiration, sometimes as justification for the opposite.***

This cluster maps the foundational texts and concepts that built the ideological architecture of the digital age. Haraway, Barlow, VNS Matrix — and the distance between what they meant and how their words are now used.

The question this cluster asks: who is using this concept, and to what end?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [[Cyborg]]
Haraway, 1985. The boundary between human and machine as a political construction. Now also a billionaire's personal brand.

### [[Situated Knowledge]]
All knowledge is produced from somewhere. There is no view from nowhere — only the pretence of one.

### [[Informatics of Domination]]
How information systems build new hierarchies. The algorithm does not discriminate. The discrimination is in the data.

### [[Digital Libertarianism]]
Barlow, 1996. The internet as a space beyond state jurisdiction. The vacuum did not stay empty.

### [[Hacker Ethic]]
Curiosity as moral duty. Knowledge as commons. The ethic that built open source — and was then absorbed by the companies it enabled.

### [[Cyberfeminism]]
VNS Matrix, 1991. Technology as terrain of gender struggle. The clitoris as direct line to the matrix.

### [[Black Box]]
The input goes in. The output comes out. What happens in between is not available to you.

### [[Obfuscation]]
Deliberately making systems opaque. A corporate strategy. Also, potentially, a tool for the less powerful.

### [[Cyborg Musk]]
The same vocabulary Haraway used to question power, redeployed to justify concentrating it.

### [[Objectivism (Rand)]]
Self-interest as moral virtue. The philosophy that Silicon Valley did not invent — but adopted.
