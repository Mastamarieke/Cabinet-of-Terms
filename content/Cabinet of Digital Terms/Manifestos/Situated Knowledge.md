***There is no view from nowhere. Every claim is made from somewhere, by someone.***

**Literal meaning:** **Situated knowledge** is **Donna Haraway**'s epistemological concept: all knowledge is produced from a specific position — a body, a location, a set of interests, a historical moment. There is no neutral, objective, view-from-nowhere. The claim to objectivity — the "god trick," as **Haraway** calls it — is itself a position, one that conceals its situatedness behind a claim to universality.

**Origin:** **Haraway** developed the concept in her 1988 essay "Situated Knowledges: The Science Question in Feminism and the Privilege of Partial Perspective," published in *Feminist Studies*. She was responding to two positions she found equally inadequate: naive realism (there is one true account of the world, accessible through correct method) and radical social constructivism (all accounts are equally constructed, therefore equally valid). **Situated knowledge** offered a third way: partial, accountable, embodied knowledge that is rigorous precisely because it acknowledges its position.

> The claim that all knowledge is produced from a specific position — and that claiming otherwise is not objectivity but a particular kind of power.

**The Appeal:** **Situated knowledge** offers a way to take both knowledge and power seriously simultaneously. Science is real; it is also produced from positions with interests. The concept does not dismiss expertise — it asks expertise to account for itself.

**The Friction:** The concept is frequently misread as relativism — the claim that all knowledge is equally valid because all is situated. This is the opposite of **Haraway**'s argument. She claims that partial, positioned, accountable knowledge is more rigorous than the false universalism it replaces. [[Black Box]] — the system that conceals its workings — is the structural embodiment of **situated knowledge**'s opposite: a claim to neutral output that conceals the inputs, decisions, and positions that produced it. [[Unblackboxing]] is the analytical practice that **situated knowledge** demands: tracing who made the knowledge, from where, and with what interests. [[Informatics of Domination]] applies the framework to information systems specifically.

**Why This Matters:** **Situated knowledge** is the epistemological foundation for most of this vault's methodology. Every term analysis asks: who coined this term, from where, and whose interests does its current use serve? That is **situated knowledge** in practice.
