# Objectivism (Rand)


***Self-interest is a virtue. Altruism is a vice. Silicon Valley found this useful.***

**Literal meaning:** Objectivism is the philosophy developed by Ayn Rand, most fully expressed in *Atlas Shrugged* (1957) and *The Virtue of Selfishness* (1964): that reason is the only valid means of knowledge, that the individual's own happiness is the moral purpose of life, that productive achievement is the highest activity, and that laissez-faire capitalism is the only social system consistent with individual rights. Altruism — acting for others — is a moral evil. Self-interest is a moral good.

**Origin:** Rand, born Alisa Zinovievna Rosenbaum in St Petersburg in 1905, developed Objectivism as a direct response to both Soviet collectivism and what she saw as the irrationalism of mainstream Western philosophy. *The Fountainhead* (1943) and *Atlas Shrugged* (1957) presented her philosophy in fictional form. Rand's direct influence on Silicon Valley has been documented extensively: Peter Thiel, Travis Kalanick (Uber), Steve Jobs, and Alan Greenspan (a member of Rand's inner circle) have all cited her influence. A 2016 survey found that *Atlas Shrugged* was the second most cited book among tech founders after the Bible.

> A philosophy of individual sovereignty and self-interest as moral virtue — the upstream ideology of much Silicon Valley culture.

**The Appeal:** Objectivism addresses a real experience: the frustration of the exceptional individual constrained by mediocre institutions. For ambitious, technically talented people who feel that conventional systems undervalue them, Rand's moral validation of exceptional self-interest is genuinely appealing. The framework also provides cover for self-serving decisions: what you are doing is not extraction — it is rational self-interest, which is moral.

**The Friction:** Objectivism provides the ideological foundation for [[Digital Libertarianism]] — the internet as a space where exceptional individuals create without state interference. [[Muskism]] is its most public contemporary expression: the exceptional individual whose genius entitles him to operate outside normal constraints. [[Technofeudalism]] is what Rand's vision produces when a small number of exceptional individuals achieve platform dominance: not the competitive capitalism she advocated but feudal concentration. [[Fortress Futurism]] — technology as exclusive refuge for those who can afford to exit — is Objectivism applied to catastrophe.

**Why This Matters:** Objectivism names the moral philosophy that makes extractive platform capitalism feel like virtue. Once you see it in operation — in how founders talk about disruption, in how taxation is discussed, in how "parasites" are identified — a great deal of tech culture becomes readable as philosophy, not just business strategy.
