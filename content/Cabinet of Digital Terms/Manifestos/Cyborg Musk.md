***He merges his identity with his machines. This is presented as vision. It is also a brand.***

**Literal meaning:** **Cyborg Musk** describes **Elon Musk**'s deployment of **cyborg** imagery — through **Neuralink**'s **brain-computer interface**, SpaceX's "multi-planetary species" rhetoric, and his self-presentation as the leading edge of human-machine fusion — as a personal brand and ideological position.

**Origin:** The term was developed within Project Digitale Alertheid as an analytical object: the name Musk has used for himself on Tesla documentation is "Technoking," and his public persona consistently positions him at the human-machine boundary. **Neuralink**'s stated goal is to merge human cognition with AI. The language is explicitly posthumanist — drawn from science fiction and from **Haraway**'s **cyborg** tradition, but evacuated of its political content.

> The appropriation of **Haraway**'s **cyborg** metaphor for a personal and commercial brand — where the boundary-disrupting figure becomes a marketing identity.

**The Appeal:** The **cyborg** mythology is compelling. The idea of a human being who is also something more — faster, smarter, transcending biological limits — has deep cultural roots. Musk's deployment of this mythology is effective: it positions him as visionary, as posthuman avant-garde, as someone operating at a scale of ambition that ordinary frameworks cannot contain.

**The Friction:** **Haraway**'s cyborg was a figure of boundary disruption that challenged hierarchies of gender, race, and species. Musk's cyborg is a figure of enhancement that concentrates power. [[Cyborg]] questions the naturalness of the human/machine boundary; **Cyborg Musk** uses that boundary as a brand distinguishing those who have merged (the visionary tech elite) from those who have not (everyone else). [[Muskism]] — Slobodian & Tarnoff's 2026 characterisation of Musk's governance model — is what **Cyborg Musk** is the personal embodiment of: the same framework applied to states and markets. [[Transhumanism]] is the ideological ecosystem: the aspiration to transcend the human is dressed in cyborg imagery but serves a quite specific set of interests. [[Digital Libertarianism]] is the political foundation: the state should not constrain the posthuman.

**Why This Matters:** **Cyborg Musk** makes visible a general mechanism: critical vocabulary can be adopted while its critique is discarded. The aesthetics migrate; the politics do not. Once you see this move, you can identify it wherever it happens — in corporate "disruption" language, in Silicon Valley "moonshot" rhetoric, in any use of countercultural aesthetics in the service of power concentration.
