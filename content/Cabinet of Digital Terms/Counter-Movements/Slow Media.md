# Slow Media


***He reads one newspaper instead of fourteen feeds. He found out about it on Twitter.***

**Literal meaning:** **Slow media** is a concept — and loosely, a movement — advocating for deliberate, quality-focused media consumption over the speed, volume, and distraction of digital news feeds. It draws on the broader "slow" movement (slow food, slow travel) and advocates reading fewer sources more deeply, pausing before sharing, and disengaging from the real-time news cycle.

**Origin:** The term was popularised by a 2010 *Slow Media Manifesto* published by Johannes Grübl, Benedikt Köhler, and Sabria David — a short text arguing for media that is produced carefully, consumed mindfully, and valued for quality rather than speed. It circulated online, was shared extensively via social media, and was discussed on blogs and in journalism. The irony of its distribution was noted immediately.

> Deliberate, quality-focused media consumption — a practice distributed through the fast, high-volume media environment it questions.

**The Appeal:** **Slow media** addresses something real. The accelerating news cycle, the compulsion to stay updated, the fragmentation of attention — these are genuine problems. Deep reading, long-form journalism, and deliberate disengagement from breaking news produce documented wellbeing and comprehension benefits. The advice is often sound.

**The Friction:** The structural paradox is immediate. The **Slow Media** Manifesto was shared rapidly via Twitter and blogs — fast, high-volume platforms. **Slow media** content is produced and distributed through the same attention economy it opposes. [[Attention Economy]] — attention as scarce resource and commodity — profits equally from fast and slow content, as long as it generates engagement. [[Clickbait]] is what **slow media** is explicitly opposed to; they share the same distribution infrastructure. [[Digital Detox]] is **slow media**'s more radical sibling — and shares the same recuperation problem. The critique of speed becomes a content category; the content category drives engagement; the platform wins.

**Why This Matters:** **Slow media** makes visible a structural constraint on media criticism: you cannot opt out of platform distribution and still reach an audience. The movement that wants to slow down must use the same infrastructure as the movement it opposes. That constraint is worth naming.
