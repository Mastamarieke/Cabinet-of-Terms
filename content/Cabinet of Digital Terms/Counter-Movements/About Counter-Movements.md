# Counter-Movements


***The solution can be worse than the problem — especially when you need five new apps to detox from your apps.***

This cluster maps a specific mechanism: resistance that reproduces what it resists. Digital detox, slow media, mindful scrolling — promoted via the very platforms they critique. The cure sold by the disease.

But not all counter-movements are recuperated. Some name a genuine structural need — for physical presence, embodied connection, and environments free from designed distraction. The analytical work is distinguishing between the two: resistance that changes something, and resistance that generates content.

The analytical core: recuperation. The system absorbs its own criticism.

The question this cluster asks: is this resistance, or is it content about resistance?

## Terms in this cluster

### [[Digital Detox]]
Going offline as a lifestyle product. The retreat is booked online. The experience gets posted. The resistance becomes content.

### [[IRL (In Real Life)]]
Physical reality now needs a label to distinguish itself from the digital default. The counter-movements it names — cat cafés, phone-free schools, offline therapy, social prescribing for outdoor time — respond to a real deprivation. *(new V10; also in: Subcultural Vocabulary)*

### [[Mindful Scrolling]]
Paying attention to how you scroll — recommended by the attention economy itself.

### [[Slow Media]]
Consuming less, more carefully. A manifesto distributed via the media it questions.

### [[Tech-Free Challenge]]
Abstaining from technology as a viral format. Shared on the platforms you are abstaining from.

### [[Unplugging]]
Going offline as an online identity. The paradox completes itself.

Going offline as an online identity. The paradox completes itself.
