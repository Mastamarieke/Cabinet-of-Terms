# Subhuman


***The word has a history. Every time it has been used at scale, something worse followed.***

**Literal meaning:** **Subhuman** describes a person or group as less than fully human — beneath the threshold of humanity that commands moral consideration. The word and its equivalents (*untermenschen*, "animals," "vermin," "parasites") have a documented role in preparing populations for mass violence: they remove the psychological barriers against harming members of the target group.

**Origin:** The term has a documented history in genocidal rhetoric, most explicitly in Nazi ideology where *Untermenschen* was used to classify Jewish people, Roma, and others as racially inferior. Academic research by Gregory Stanton (genocide scholar), James Waller, and Ervin Staub has established dehumanising language as a predictable precursor in the sequence leading to mass atrocity. The term's circulation in contemporary digital discourse — applied to migrants, minorities, and political opponents — has been documented by researchers including Vidhya Ramalingam and organisations including the Global Network on Extremism and Technology.

> Language that positions a person or group as less than human — with a documented historical role in preparing populations for violence.

**The Appeal:** From the perspective of those who use it: the term makes extreme political positions feel justified. If the target group is genuinely **subhuman**, then policies that would otherwise be unacceptable become proportionate responses. The dehumanisation is the permission structure.

**The Friction:** [[Dehumanization]] — Bandura's moral disengagement framework — describes the psychological mechanism: once a group is categorised as less than human, the inhibitions against harming them are systematically reduced. The research is not speculative — it is reconstructed from genocide perpetrators' own accounts. Digital platforms accelerate the circulation of dehumanising language by treating it as expression rather than as the precursor stage of a documented harm sequence. [[Great Replacement]] — conspiracy theory framing migrants as existential threat — is the ideological context in which **subhuman** language currently circulates most actively. [[Deplatforming]] is the primary countermeasure; its effectiveness is contested.

**Why This Matters:** **Subhuman** is a word that functions as a stage in a sequence. Knowing the sequence — dehumanisation, persecution, mass violence — makes the word's presence in mainstream discourse a different kind of signal than general offensiveness.
