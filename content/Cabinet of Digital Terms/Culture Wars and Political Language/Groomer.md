# Groomer


***A child protection term, borrowed and applied to teachers, librarians, and politicians. The original meaning did not disappear — it was used as a weapon.***

**Literal meaning:** In child safeguarding, grooming describes the deliberate process by which an adult builds trust with a child to facilitate sexual abuse. In contemporary political discourse, "**groomer**" has been repurposed as a label applied to LGBTQ+ people, educators, and others accused of exposing children to LGBTQ+ content or identities — implying that such exposure constitutes abuse.

**Origin:** The "**groomer**" smear is a rhetorical strategy that falsely accuses LGBTQ+ individuals and allies of child abuse, perpetuating historical fear-mongering that has caused immense harm, as **Cassisa (2023)** demonstrates in her analysis of the long history of these accusations. While the safeguarding definition of grooming has been in professional and legal use since the 1990s to describe the systematic process by which adults build trust with children for sexual exploitation (**Whittle & Hamilton-Giachritsis, 2013**), the term's political redeployment accelerated sharply in the United States from 2021–2022, coinciding with debates over school curricula, library books, and LGBTQ+ visibility. **Caraballo (2022)** documents how this ambiguity between the legitimate safeguarding definition and the politicized smear enabled widespread harassment while providing plausible deniability for those deploying the rhetoric. By detaching the term from its factual meaning and applying it to entire communities, the smear obscures the severity of genuine crimes and paves the way for discrimination and violence.

> A child protection term semantically captured and redeployed as a political weapon — where the original meaning is borrowed to grant authority to the attack.

**The Appeal:** The invocation of child safety is rhetorically powerful and emotionally legitimate. Genuine concern for children's wellbeing is real and widespread. The conflation of LGBTQ+ visibility with grooming exploits this concern by associating an uncontested harm (child abuse) with a contested political position (LGBTQ+ rights). The term asks nothing analytical of the person using it — the accusation carries the weight of the original meaning.

**The Friction:** Semantic capture — the use of an authoritative term from one domain to delegitimise a target in another — is the mechanism. [[Dehumanization]] research shows that applying criminal or predatory labels to groups systematically lowers the threshold for violence against them. The term's ambiguity is functional: platforms struggle to moderate "**groomer**" accusations because the safeguarding meaning is legitimate. [[Online Grooming]] — the actual practice of child abuse facilitation — is the real-world harm the term was designed to name; its political redeployment both exploits and obscures that harm. [[Social Contagion]] shares structural features: both apply pathology or predator frames to LGBTQ+ identity.

**Why This Matters:** **Groomer** makes visible how child safety discourse can be weaponised — and how semantic ambiguity serves as both rhetorical weapon and moderation shield. Once you see the capture, the invocation of child safety is never automatically authoritative.
