***The identity is entirely defined by what it opposes. Remove the target and there is nothing left.***

**Literal meaning:** **Anti-woke** describes a political and cultural stance that positions itself in opposition to "woke" ideology — progressive positions on race, gender, identity, and institutional reform. The term defines itself negatively: its content is the negation of whatever "woke" is currently understood to mean.

**Origin:** "Woke" originated in Black American vernacular as a term for social and political awareness, particularly regarding racial injustice. It was adopted by progressive movements and then, from around 2016, began to be used pejoratively by critics to describe what they saw as excessive political correctness. **Anti-woke** emerged as its mirror: a reactive identity constituted entirely by opposition to the perceived excesses of woke politics.

> A political identity constituted entirely by opposition — where the content of the position is defined entirely by what it opposes.

**The Appeal:** **Anti-woke** addresses genuine frustrations. Concerns about free speech, institutional overreach, ideological conformity, and the policing of language are held by people across the political spectrum — not only by those on the right. The **anti-woke** frame captures a real experience of cultural pressure and provides community around shared resistance.

**The Friction:** The reactive structure is the analytical core. An identity defined entirely by opposition has no positive content of its own — it cannot be falsified, because its target constantly shifts. When "woke" changes meaning, **anti-woke** changes with it. [[Woke Mind Virus]] is the pathologising intensification of the same stance. [[Dehumanization]] research — Bandura's moral disengagement framework — shows that reactive identities constituted around opposition to a perceived enemy are particularly susceptible to escalation: the enemy must be continually redefined as more extreme to maintain the identity's coherence. [[Flood the Zone]] — information overload as strategic destabilisation — is a tactic that benefits from **anti-woke** reactions: the more the opposition is activated, the more engagement the platform generates.

**Why This Matters:** **Anti-woke** makes visible what happens when a political identity is constituted entirely by negation. The movement cannot win, because winning would dissolve the identity. The opposition is the point.
