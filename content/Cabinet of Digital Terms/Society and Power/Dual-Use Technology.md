# Dual-Use Technology


***The GPS in your phone was developed by the military. The AI writing your emails is training drone targeting.***

**Literal meaning:** **Dual-use technology** describes technology that has both legitimate civilian applications and potential military or weapons-related applications — where the same capability serves different purposes depending on who deploys it and to what end. The concept is central to export control law, arms control policy, and technology governance.

**Origin:** The concept is established in international law — the EU Dual-Use Regulation (2021) and US Export Administration Regulations govern the export of technologies with military applications. It applies historically to nuclear materials, precision manufacturing, and chemical precursors. In the digital age, the concept extends to AI systems, geospatial data, facial recognition, encryption, and communications infrastructure — where the civilian and military applications are often indistinguishable at the product level.

> Technologies developed for civilian purposes that have direct military applications — where the boundary between them is structurally porous, not incidentally crossed.

**The Appeal:** Most powerful technologies are dual-use by nature. Restricting **dual-use technology** restricts both applications simultaneously — including beneficial ones. Export controls and governance frameworks attempt to manage the military dimension without prohibiting the civilian one; the difficulty of that distinction is part of what makes the governance so contested.

**The Friction:** [[Digital-Military-Industrial Complex]] is the structural context: the entanglement of tech and defence makes dual-use not an edge case but the normal condition of advanced technology.  is a current example: **Niantic**'s LGM, trained on Pokémon Go player data, has civilian mapping applications and military spatial intelligence applications — and the boundary between them is the subject of McClendon's "I could definitely see it" non-answer. [[Revolving Door]] personnel make dual-use governance more complex: regulators who have worked in defence companies regulate dual-use with institutional relationships that shape their judgments.

**Why This Matters:** **Dual-use technology** names the structural porosity of the civilian/military distinction in advanced technology. Once you see it, "this is a civilian product" is not a sufficient answer to the question of what it enables. The follow-up question is always: by whom, and to what end?
