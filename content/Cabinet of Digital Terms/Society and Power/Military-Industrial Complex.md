# Military-Industrial Complex


***Eisenhower warned about it in his farewell address. The industry he described has since absorbed the internet.***

> Eisenhower's term for the entanglement of the military and the defence industry — still running, now with software, AI, and Big Tech as core components.

**Literal meaning:** The **military-industrial complex** describes the structural relationship between the military establishment, the defence industry, and the political system — in which each reinforces the others' interests: the military requires ever more advanced weapons; the industry profits from producing them; the political system depends on the industry's economic contribution and the military's institutional power.

**Origin:** The term was introduced by Dwight D. Eisenhower in his 1961 farewell address as US President: "In the councils of government, we must guard against the acquisition of unwarranted influence, whether sought or unsought, by the **military-industrial complex**." The warning came from a former five-star general who had commanded Allied forces in Europe — making it the most internally credible critique of the relationship the US political system has ever produced.

>** Military-Industrial Complex** — originally Eisenhower’s term for the alliance between the military, government, and defense industry; increasingly expanded to describe contemporary software, AI, cloud, surveillance, and platform infrastructures in which technology companies function simultaneously as civilian services, military contractors, and governance systems.

**The Appeal:** Defence industries produce genuine capabilities that genuine security requires. The structural relationship Eisenhower described is a natural consequence of any society that requires a standing military: someone must produce the equipment, and whoever produces it will have commercial interests in its continued procurement.

**The Friction:** Eisenhower's warning was about the structural consequence: that the relationship produces pressure for military expenditure that is not purely strategic but is also commercial and political. [[Reconstruction Economy]] is one documented consequence: markets that depend on continued conflict for their existence. [[Revolving Door]] is the personnel mechanism: individuals moving between military, government, and defence industry positions carry relationships that serve all three institutions. [[Digital-Military-Industrial Complex]] is the extension: the same structural relationship with Big Tech replacing or supplementing traditional defence contractors.

**Why This Matters:** **Military-industrial complex** names a structural entanglement that persists regardless of the intentions of its participants. Eisenhower's point was not that the participants are corrupt but that the structure produces systematic pressure. Once you see the structure, individual decisions become legible as responses to incentives that are not primarily strategic.
