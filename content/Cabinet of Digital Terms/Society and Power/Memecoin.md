# Memecoin


***The coin was a joke. People lost real money. The person who created it did not.***

**Literal meaning:** A **memecoin** is a cryptocurrency whose value is based primarily on cultural narrative, community, and speculation rather than any underlying technology or use case. The name acknowledges the meme — internet cultural unit — as the primary value driver. Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, and the TRUMP and MELANIA coins launched in January 2025 are examples.

**Origin:** Dogecoin (2013) was the original **memecoin** — launched as a joke based on the Doge meme, it acquired a community and a market capitalisation. The category scaled with the 2020–2021 crypto boom. The TRUMP **memecoin** launched days before Donald Trump's January 2025 inauguration, accumulating billions in market capitalisation and making Trump and affiliated holders significant financial beneficiaries — while retail investors who bought at peak prices lost substantial sums when the price declined. The event demonstrated **memecoin** as a political financial instrument.

> A financial instrument whose value is explicitly cultural rather than fundamental — combining speculation, community formation, and the potential for coordinated manipulation.

**The Appeal:** Memecoins offer community participation with financial upside. The cultural narrative is real and engaging; the community is real; the possibility of large returns is real for early participants. For retail investors who feel excluded from conventional financial markets, memecoins offer accessible speculation with genuine entertainment value.

**The Friction:** The financial structure is systematically advantageous for early holders and disadvantageous for later ones. [[Grifters]] is the relevant category: the community provides the trust that the financial extraction requires. [[Financial Fabulism]] is the genre: future-value narrative that cannot be evaluated by conventional financial metrics. The TRUMP **memecoin**'s specific harm was political: a sitting president's financial interests were directly tied to the price of a speculative instrument he promoted — a conflict of interest with no precedent in democratic governance. [[Broligarchy]] is the social network through which **memecoin** promotions circulate: the same **influencer** network that amplifies political positions also amplifies financial instruments.

**Why This Matters:** **Memecoin** makes visible the convergence of cultural community, financial speculation, and political power. Once you see the structure — community as distribution network for financial extraction — the question is not "is this a scam?" but "whose financial interests are served by the community's belief?"
