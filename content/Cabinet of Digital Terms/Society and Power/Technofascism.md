# Technofascism


***The historical reference is deliberate.***

**Literal meaning:** **Technofascism** describes the fusion of advanced technology infrastructure with authoritarian political power — a political formation in which Big Tech companies, their owners, and authoritarian political actors become mutually reinforcing, using technology for surveillance, control, information dominance, and the elimination of opposition.

**Origin:** The term has been used in critical technology studies and political theory. Philosopher Mark Coeckelbergh deployed it as an analytical political diagnosis in 2026, distinguishing it from historical fascism while identifying structural similarities: the displacement of democratic governance by a combination of authoritarian political power and private corporate control, the use of information systems for population management and opposition suppression, and the ideological fusion of technological progress with political dominance.

> The fusion of authoritarian political power with advanced technology infrastructure — a new political category for a new political formation.

**The Appeal:** The term has analytical precision that softer descriptions lack. When a political leader controls communications infrastructure, uses algorithmic systems to suppress opposition, and deploys state resources to benefit allied tech companies — calling this something other than a fusion of authoritarian and technological power understates what is happening.

**The Friction:** The term carries strong rhetorical charge and can be deployed loosely. The analytical value depends on specificity: what distinguishes **technofascism** from ordinary authoritarianism is the specific role of technology in enabling population management at scales that pre-digital authoritarianism could not achieve, and the specific role of private tech companies as political actors rather than merely commercial ones. [[Muskism]] is the transitional form: private infrastructure accumulation that has acquired political influence without yet achieving state coercive power. [[Cybernetic Governance]] is the administrative mechanism. [[Flood the Zone]] is the information management strategy.

**Why This Matters:** **Technofascism** names a political endpoint on a trajectory that is visible in present conditions. Once you have the term, the question is not whether it has arrived but at what point on the path from **Muskism** to **technofascism** we currently stand — and what governance structures, if any, are capable of interruption.
