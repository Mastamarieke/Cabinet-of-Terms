# Hubris Syndrome


***He stopped listening. Not suddenly — gradually, as the accountability structures were removed one by one.***

**Literal meaning:** **Hubris syndrome** is a clinical and political concept developed by neurologist and former British Foreign Secretary David Owen, describing a condition produced by the accumulation of power: a pattern of grandiosity, contempt for others, loss of contact with reality, and identification of one's own interests with the interests of humanity — emerging specifically from extended periods of unchecked authority.

**Origin:** Owen developed the concept in _The **Hubris Syndrome**: Bush, Blair and the Intoxication of Power_ (2007; expanded 2018), drawing on his experience as both a neurologist and former politician. He argues that **hubris syndrome** is not a pre-existing personality disorder, but a condition produced by power itself — emerging when accountability structures weaken and leaders are continuously reinforced in their sense of exceptionalism.

> A condition produced by sustained, unchecked power — where leaders become progressively detached from feedback, consequence, and reality.

**The Appeal:** **Hubris syndrome** provides a vocabulary for a pattern that is widely recognised but rarely named with clinical precision: the leader who was once effective and became catastrophically overconfident. The concept has explanatory power for historical and contemporary political failures.

**The Friction:** In the digital context, **hubris syndrome** is relevant to the specific conditions of tech billionaire power: extreme wealth accumulated without the accountability of electoral politics, boards that have been captured or neutralised, media relationships that provide amplification rather than scrutiny. [[Founder Mode]] — Graham's argument for founder authority above governance — describes the structural conditions that produce **hubris syndrome** by removing accountability. [[Techno King]] is one self-expression of the condition. [[Muskism]] names the governance model it produces when the hubristic actor also accumulates political infrastructure.

**Why This Matters:** **Hubris syndrome** names a predictable consequence of unchecked power — not an individual character flaw. Once you see it as structural rather than personal, the question shifts from "why does this person behave this way?" to "what accountability structures were removed, and when?"
