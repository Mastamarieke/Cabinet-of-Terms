# Narrative Engineering

***The story was not true. It was not meant to be believed. It was meant to be repeated.***

**Literal meaning**
*Narrative* — a structured account of events that produces meaning. *Engineering* — deliberate, systematic construction. **Narrative Engineering** is the professional-grade construction and deployment of political stories designed to shift public perception.

**Origin**
Wardle and Derakhshan (2017) established the framework of information disorder, distinguishing misinformation, disinformation, and malinformation by intent. Benkler, Faris, and Roberts (2018) documented in *Network Propaganda* how **narrative engineering** operates as a sustained, coordinated infrastructure — not isolated lies but interconnected story systems designed to make certain explanations feel natural and others feel unthinkable.

> The deliberate construction and platform-mediated distribution of political narratives — not individual lies but coordinated story architectures designed to shape what feels true.

**The Appeal**
Strategic communication and messaging are legitimate tools of political life. **Narrative Engineering** names the professional expertise that makes political communication effective.

**The Friction**
The distinction between communication and engineering is the scale and systematicity. [[Flood the Zone]] is the volume tactic; **Narrative Engineering** is the architectural layer: building the frames within which flooding operates. Wardle and Derakhshan (2017) document that the most effective disinformation does not assert falsehoods but arranges truths into misleading architectures. [[Bot Farms]] provide the distribution infrastructure; [[Synthetic Generation]] provides the content at scale.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see **Narrative Engineering** as infrastructure rather than persuasion, you see that the battle is not about individual facts — it is about which frames make facts legible, and who builds those frames.
