# Muskism


***This is not about one man. It is about what one man's accumulation of infrastructure reveals about the system that allowed it.***

> A governance model in which private infrastructure, public dependence, and personal authority combine — without the accountability structures that normally accompany power at this scale.

**Literal meaning:** **Muskism** is Quinn Slobodian and Ben Tarnoff's term, introduced in 2026, for the governance model represented by **Elon Musk**: the accumulation of critical public infrastructure under private control, the use of that control to extract political influence, and the deployment of that influence to further reduce the regulatory constraints on private power.

**Origin:** Slobodian and Tarnoff published "**Muskism**" as an analytical concept to name a governance logic that preceded Musk but found its clearest embodiment in him: private ownership of communications infrastructure (X/Twitter), space access infrastructure (SpaceX), payment infrastructure (X Money), cognitive interface infrastructure (**Neuralink**), and direct government advisory roles — a concentration of infrastructure dependency that has no democratic accountability.

>**Muskism** A governance model in which critical public infrastructure is consolidated under private ownership, that ownership is transformed into political influence, and that influence is then used to shield private power from democratic accountability.

**The Appeal:** **Muskism** offers an argument for its own legitimacy: the private sector builds better infrastructure faster, exceptional individuals generate more value than bureaucratic institutions, and regulatory constraints on innovation damage public welfare. These arguments have empirical support in specific contexts. The question is whether they justify the governance outcome.

**The Friction:** [[Technofeudalism]] is the economic model; **Muskism** is the political form it takes when the cloud lords acquire state power. [[Objectivism (Rand)]] provides the moral framework: the exceptional individual's interests align with humanity's interests. [[Broligarchy]] is the social network: **Muskism** is not a solo project but a class formation. [[Technofascism]] is the more extreme adjacent concept: **Muskism** that has acquired state coercive power and abandoned any pretence of democratic legitimacy. [[Flood the Zone]] is the communication strategy: the information environment is too saturated to sustain accountability.

**Why This Matters:** **Muskism** names a governance logic, not a personality. Once you see it as a model rather than an individual, you can identify it wherever the same accumulation of infrastructure dependency, political influence, and accountability evasion appears.
