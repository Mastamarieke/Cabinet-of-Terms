***The ideology is the product. The followers are the market.***

**Literal meaning:** **Grifters** are con artists — people who extract money or resources from marks through deception, false promises, and manufactured trust. In contemporary political and tech culture, the term describes actors who combine personal financial extraction with ideological performance: the con is accompanied by a world-view, a community, and a set of beliefs that make the extraction feel like participation in a movement.

**Origin:** The term comes from criminal slang for confidence tricksters: “the grift” is the scam, and the “grifter” the operator. In contemporary political and online culture, it describes figures who monetise ideology, outrage, insider knowledge, or identity through performances of authenticity aimed at extracting money, attention, loyalty, or influence from followers.

> Con artists who perform ideological authenticity — where the belief system is the product and the followers are the market.

**The Appeal:** Grifter operations are effective because the ideological component provides genuine community, meaning, and belonging. The followers are not simply deceived — they are members of something. The best grifts provide real value alongside the extraction: real entertainment, real information, real connection — which makes the deception harder to see.

**The Friction:** [[Financial Fabulism]] is the respectable terminus of the same continuum: the genre conventions of future-value narrative that can cover fraud. [[Memecoin]] is the financial instrument: cryptocurrency speculation explicitly based on cultural narrative rather than underlying value, where the community around the coin is the product being sold. [[Reconstruction Economy]] provides the operational context: high-value, low-oversight environments with complex accountability structures that grift operations systematically exploit. The ideological veneer — "this is about freedom, disruption, or saving the country" — is the mechanism that converts financial extraction into community participation.

**Why This Matters:** **Grifters** names the specific harm of ideological cover for financial extraction. Once you see the pattern — manifesto + extraction + community attribution of losses — you can identify it across political movements, cryptocurrency projects, and **influencer** businesses.
