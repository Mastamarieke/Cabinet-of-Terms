# Fortress Futurism


***The wall is not against the past. It is for a future available only to some.***

**Literal meaning:** **Fortress futurism** describes the ideology and aesthetic of using advanced technology to create protected enclaves — private islands, underground bunkers, orbital habitats, off-grid compounds — for a wealthy elite while the external world degrades. It combines the survivalist impulse with the resources to engineer escape.

**Origin:** The term was popularised by journalist Douglas Rushkoff in _Survival of the Richest_ (2022), where he describes tech billionaires preparing for social collapse through fortified compounds, private security, and technological control systems. Closely connected to elite **prepperism**, **Fortress Futurism** treats collapse as something to survive individually rather than solve collectively. Critics link this mindset to Silicon Valley libertarianism, defense-tech culture, **cybernetic governance**, and infrastructural power concentrated among the ultra-rich, “broligarch” tech elites, and platform companies.

> The use of wealth and technology to engineer exit from shared social conditions — where the exceptional few build walls against the consequences affecting everyone else.

**The Appeal:** If you genuinely believe civilisational collapse is probable, preparation is rational. The billionaire's compound is just a well-resourced version of a concern that many people share. For those with the means, the question of how to protect your family from catastrophic risk is not unreasonable.

**The Friction:** The exit logic has political consequences for those left behind. If the wealthy and powerful can engineer escape from civilisational risk, their political incentive to prevent that risk diminishes. [[Longtermism]] provides the moral cover: the important thing is to preserve a civilisational remnant, even if the path involves abandoning the majority. [[Mars Colonization]] is the most ambitious form: exit from the planet itself. [[Muskism]] is what happens when the fortress builders also acquire political power: not just exit, but governance of the exit infrastructure. The apartheid connection is not coincidental: apartheid South Africa developed sophisticated infrastructure for managing populations in conditions of structural inequality — skills now marketed to tech security firms.

**Why This Matters:** **Fortress futurism** names the political logic of elite exit. Once you see it, the billionaire's bunker is not eccentric — it is a rational response to a world whose risks they have helped create and whose costs they are engineering not to pay.
