***The old world must end before the new one can begin. This belief recurs across centuries.***

**Literal meaning:** **Millenarianism** describes the belief in an imminent, total, collective transformation of society — typically through catastrophe — that will produce a new, purified order. The term derives from Christian apocalyptic traditions expecting a thousand-year reign of the saints, but the structure (corrupt present → catastrophic transition → redeemed community) recurs across religious, secular, and political movements throughout history.

**Origin:** In _The Pursuit of the Millennium_ (1957), Norman Cohn showed how many radical movements throughout history followed the same basic pattern: society is seen as corrupt and collapsing, catastrophe is believed to be approaching, and a small group imagines itself as the one that will survive, rebuild, or reveal the truth. Michael Barkun later traced similar dynamics in modern conspiracy culture and extremist politics in _A Culture of Conspiracy_ (2003), including movements built around hidden enemies, secret knowledge, and apocalyptic expectations.

Today, versions of this structure appear across very different ideologies — from conspiracy movements like **QAnon** to accelerationist tech culture and certain forms of political extremism. Even when these movements present themselves as secular or technological, they often repeat the emotional and narrative patterns of older religious end-times movements.

> The recurring pattern in which the present is seen as hopelessly corrupt, a major rupture is imminent, and a purified world will follow.

**The Appeal:** **Millenarianism** is cognitively and emotionally powerful. It provides: a diagnosis of the present (corrupt, failing), an explanation of suffering (persecution of the chosen), a community of shared belief, and a future vindication. The structure is available for any political content — left, right, religious, secular — which is why it recurs so reliably.

**The Friction:** The millenarian structure does ideological work by making present action feel cosmically justified. [[Armageddon Complex]] is the power-accumulation form: the emergency is real and therefore extraordinary measures are warranted. [[Endtime Fascism]] is the authoritarian endpoint. [[Disaster Nationalism]] borrows the millenarian structure without the explicit religious content. [[QAnon]] is a contemporary millenarian movement: the Great Awakening as transition event, the **deep state** as corrupt present, the Q community as chosen remnant. Cohn's historical insight was that millenarian movements consistently target the same populations for violence during their transition moments — those identified as agents of the corrupt present.

**Why This Matters:** **Millenarianism** names the template, not the content. Once you can identify the structure — corrupt present, catastrophic transition, chosen community — you can see it operating across very different political and religious contexts, and ask: who is identified as the agent of corruption, and what actions does the transition moment justify?
