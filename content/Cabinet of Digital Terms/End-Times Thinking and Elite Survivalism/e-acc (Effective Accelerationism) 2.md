# e/acc (Effective Accelerationism)


***Accelerate everything. Safety concerns are obstacles. The faster we go, the better the outcome. Probably.***

**Literal meaning:** Effective Accelerationism (e/acc) is an ideological movement, primarily circulating in tech culture from around 2022–2023, that advocates for the maximal acceleration of technological development — particularly AI — without restriction. Safety concerns, regulatory frameworks, and alignment research are reframed as obstacles to progress that will ultimately benefit humanity. The faster the technology develops, the sooner the good outcomes arrive.

**Origin:** e/acc — short for “effective accelerationism” — emerged online in 2022–2023 as a reaction against AI safety culture and the more cautious approach associated with effective altruism. The movement developed largely through Twitter/X, where anonymous and pseudonymous accounts promoted the idea that technological progress, especially in artificial intelligence, should move faster rather than slower. One of the most visible early advocates was “Beff Jezos,” the online persona of former Google researcher Guillaume Verdon.

Supporters of e/acc argue that technological acceleration is the main driver of human advancement and that excessive regulation, ethical caution, or “doomer” narratives risk slowing civilisation itself. The movement mixes Silicon Valley futurism, internet meme culture, transhumanist ideas, and the influence of accelerationist philosophy associated with thinkers such as Nick Land. In practice, e/acc often frames technological development as an unstoppable force that should be embraced rather than restrained.

As the term spread online in 2023, it became both an ideology and an aesthetic: ironic usernames, hyper-optimistic AI rhetoric, techno-capitalist ambition, and a deliberately provocative rejection of caution. Critics argue that this mindset can minimise legitimate concerns about safety, labour disruption, surveillance, and political power in the race to build more powerful technologies.

> An ideology that treats rapid technological progress as inherently beneficial — where the acceleration is the goal, and consequences are a secondary question.

**The Appeal:** e/acc speaks to a real frustration: that safety-focused discourse around AI can function as a brake on technology that could address real human problems. The argument that excessive caution costs lives by delaying beneficial technology is not trivially wrong. The movement also provides community and identity for people who feel dismissed by safety-focused discourse.

**The Friction:** e/acc performs the same ideological move as [[Doomerism]] from the opposite direction: both treat the technological future as something that happens to humanity rather than something that humanity governs. [[Longtermism]] is the philosophical cousin — both orient around transformative technological futures; e/acc removes the safety constraint. [[Muskism]] and e/acc overlap significantly: the accumulation of technological infrastructure without governance is what both advocate. [[Technofascism]] is one documented endpoint: accelerationism without constraint, at political scale. The "effective" in e/acc is a deliberate echo of "effective altruism" — reframing acceleration as a moral position rather than an interest.

**Why This Matters:** e/acc names the ideological position that treats technological safety as the primary risk — inverting the standard analysis. Once you see it as an ideology rather than a technical position, you can ask: who benefits from the removal of safety constraints, and what is the "probably" in "probably good outcomes" doing?
