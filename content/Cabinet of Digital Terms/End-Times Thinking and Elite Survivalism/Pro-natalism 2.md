# Pro-natalism


***The birthrate is falling. This is presented as a crisis. The solution is more children — from the right families.***

**Literal meaning:** **Pro-natalism** is the ideological position that encourages higher birth rates — framing reproduction as a civic or civilisational duty rather than a personal choice. In its contemporary tech-billionaire form, it combines demographic anxiety (declining birth rates in wealthy countries) with eugenicist undertones (the "right" people reproducing) and political urgency (civilisation is at stake).

**Origin:** **Pro-natalism** has historical roots in 19th and 20th century population politics — including explicitly eugenic programmes in multiple countries. Its contemporary form circulates in two distinct streams: a religious-conservative stream that frames large families as moral and civilisational duty, and a tech-billionaire stream associated with figures including **Elon Musk** (who has publicly promoted high birth rates and has multiple children by multiple partners) and Bryan Johnson. The two streams share demographic anxiety but differ in their framing of which populations should reproduce.

> An ideology that frames reproduction as civilisational duty — where demographic anxiety conceals assumptions about which populations should be reproducing.

**The Appeal:** **Pro-natalism** addresses real demographic trends. Fertility rates in many wealthy countries have declined below replacement level. This has documented consequences for pension systems, labour markets, and economic growth. Taking long-term demographic trends seriously is not inherently ideological — it is responsive to real data.

**The Friction:** The ideological loading is in the word "right." **Pro-natalism** consistently, if sometimes implicitly, specifies which populations' birth rates it is concerned about — and which it is not. [[Great Replacement]] theory is the paranoid extreme of the same demographic anxiety. [[Tradwife]] is the identity form that pro-natalist ideology promotes for women. [[Purity Culture]] shares the political concern with controlled reproduction. The tech-billionaire variant adds a transhumanist dimension: the exceptional individuals who should reproduce are those with the genetic or financial endowments to produce the next generation of exceptional individuals. The question "more children" always requires the supplementary question: whose?

**Why This Matters:** **Pro-natalism** makes visible how demographic data becomes ideological when filtered through prior assumptions about whose reproduction matters. Once you ask "whose children?" the neutral demographic framing dissolves into a political position.
