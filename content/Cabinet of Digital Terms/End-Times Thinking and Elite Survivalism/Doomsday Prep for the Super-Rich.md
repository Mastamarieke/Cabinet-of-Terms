# Doomsday Prep for the Super-Rich


***The bunker is in New Zealand. The staff have been hired but not told what for.***

**Literal meaning:** **Doomsday prep for the super-rich** describes the documented practice of tech billionaires and ultra-wealthy individuals investing in survival infrastructure — underground bunkers, remote properties, private airstrips, private security systems, stored supplies, and escape routes — in preparation for civilisational collapse scenarios.

**Origin:** Douglas Rushkoff documented the phenomenon in *Survival of the Richest* (2022), describing how he was invited to advise an anonymous group of wealthy tech investors on post-collapse survival logistics — which countries to flee to, how to maintain loyalty from armed security staff, which technologies would survive an EMP. New Zealand emerged as a favoured destination: remote, stable, English-speaking, with strict gun control. The New Zealand government subsequently introduced visa restrictions partly in response to wealthy foreign buyers purchasing land for retreat properties.

> The conversion of civilisational collapse anxiety into a private logistics project — where the appropriate response to systemic failure is individual escape.

**The Appeal:** If you genuinely believe that civilisational collapse is probable, preparation is rational — and people with enormous resources can afford preparation that others cannot. The anxiety itself is not unreasonable: climate change, political instability, and nuclear risk are real. The wealthy are not wrong to take long-term risk seriously.

**The Friction:** The political logic of elite escape is the analytical core. [[Fortress Futurism]] is the broader ideology; doomsday prep is its practical implementation. The collapse is framed as a logistics problem — where to go, what to stock, how to control the guards — rather than a political failure that might be prevented. [[Prepperism]] is the mass-market version of the same impulse; **doomsday prep for the super-rich** is prepperism at the scale of private jets and underground compounds. [[Longtermism]] provides the moral cover: preserving civilisational capability in selected individuals is more important than preventing the collapse. Rushkoff's crucial observation: the billionaires asking his advice were not asking how to prevent collapse — they had accepted it as given and were asking how to survive it.

**Why This Matters:** **Doomsday prep for the super-rich** makes visible the political decision embedded in the logistics question. Once you see the framing choice — collapse as logistics, not politics — the bunker in New Zealand is also a statement about whose survival counts as civilisational continuity.
