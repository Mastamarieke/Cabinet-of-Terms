***The world is ending. Only I can navigate what comes next. Therefore, what I accumulate is not for me — it is for everyone.***

This cluster maps ideologies that take civilisational collapse as given and build a power or identity project on top of it. The apocalypse as premise. The bunker as conclusion.

The question this cluster asks: who benefits from the end of the world being inevitable?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [[e-acc (Effective Accelerationism)]]
Accelerate everything. Safety concerns are obstacles. The faster we go, the better the outcome. Probably.

### [[Seasteading]]
Build a platform in international waters where regulations do not apply. Funded by Peter Thiel. No seastead yet exists.

### [[Doomsday Prep for the Super-Rich]]
Bunkers in New Zealand. Private airstrips. The collapse as a logistics problem, not a political failure.

### [[Dark MAGA]]
The aesthetic subculture that started as irony. Then the irony became earnest. Then people stopped taking off the costume.

### [[Disaster Nationalism]]
Using genuine crises to consolidate nationalist power. The disaster as resource, not problem.

### [[Pro-natalism]]
Demographics as ideology. Have more children — the right children. Civilisation depends on it.

### [[Millenarianism]]
End-times thinking as political-religious structure. The corrupt present, the catastrophic transition, the chosen community. Every era has its version.

### [[Prepperism]]
Survival as identity project. The stockpile as statement. The collapse that never quite arrives.

### [[Endtime Fascism]]
Fascism legitimised by eschatological urgency. The situation is so dire that ordinary limits no longer apply.

### [[Armageddon Complex]]
Apocalyptic belief that justifies power accumulation. The emergency is real. It is also convenient.
