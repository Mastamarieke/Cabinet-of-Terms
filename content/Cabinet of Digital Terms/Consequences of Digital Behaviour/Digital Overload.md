***You have read everything and retained nothing.***

**Literal meaning**
*Digital* — relating to digital technology. *Overload* — a state of excess that impairs functioning. **Digital Overload** is the condition in which the volume and pace of digital information exceed an individual's capacity to process, evaluate, or act on it.

**Origin**
Information overload as a concept precedes digital technology — Alvin Toffler described it in *Future Shock* (1970). Bawden and Robinson (2020) established the academic framework for digital information overload, documenting its causes in platform design, notification architecture, and always-on connectivity.

> The impairment of cognitive function caused by more digital information arriving than can be processed — a structural condition, not a personal one.

**The Appeal**
Being connected, informed, and responsive is genuinely valuable. **Digital overload** is partly the cost of participation in a networked world.

**The Friction**
Eppler and Mengis (2004) established that information overload impairs decision quality, reduces productivity, and increases stress — consequences that compound over time. Platform design deliberately maximises information flow: notifications, feeds, and alerts are optimised for engagement, not for cognitive wellbeing. [[Doomscrolling|Doomscrolling]] and [[Cognitive Surrender]] are documented endpoints of sustained overload.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see **Digital Overload** as a designed condition rather than a personal productivity failure, you see that the solution is not better time management — it is different design.
