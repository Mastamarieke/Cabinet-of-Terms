***You searched the symptom. An hour later you had three diagnoses, all serious.***

**Literal meaning**
*Cyber* — relating to the internet. *Chondria* — from hypochondria: unfounded preoccupation with illness. **Cyberchondria** is health anxiety escalated through repeated online symptom searching.

**Origin**
Starcevic and Berle established **cyberchondria** as a clinical concept in 2013, distinguishing it from hypochondria by its specific mechanism: the internet search loop. Unlike hypochondria, which is driven by internal anxiety, **cyberchondria** is driven by the architecture of search engines and health information platforms that surface alarming results and invite further searching.

> Health anxiety that escalates through repeated online symptom searching — driven by algorithms that surface alarming results because they generate engagement.

**The Appeal**
Searching health information online is rational: it is free, immediate, and can surface genuinely useful information. The majority of health searches are benign.

**The Friction**
Starcevic and Berle (2021) document that **cyberchondria** intensified during COVID-19 as health searching increased massively. The mechanism is structural: search algorithms surface the most alarming content because it generates the most engagement. **Recommender systems** optimised for attention retention are poorly suited to health information contexts where reassurance, not engagement, is the appropriate goal.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see **cyberchondria** as an algorithmic design outcome rather than individual anxiety, you see that the platform profiting from the health search loop is indifferent to whether the information helps or harms the user.
