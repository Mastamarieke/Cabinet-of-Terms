# AI-Specific Terms


***AI systems produce convincing text, agree with whatever you say, and gradually make certain skills feel unnecessary. None of this is accidental.***

This cluster maps the vocabulary you need to understand not just what these systems can do, but what they quietly do to the people who use them.

The question this cluster asks: what do you lose when the machine thinks for you?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [[AI Hallucination]]
The model produces a confident, detailed, completely false answer. The word "hallucination" implies a mind. There is no mind.

### [[Sycophancy (AI)]]
The model agrees with you. Not because you are right — because agreement was rewarded during training.

### [[AI-Ninja]]
The person who can make AI do anything. A romanticised identity built around a skill that may not exist in five years.

### [[AI Literacy]]
Understanding how AI works, not just how to use it. The difference between driving and knowing what is under the hood.

### [[AI Dependency]]
You could do this without AI. You no longer remember how. The tool became the infrastructure.

### [[Cognitive Offloading]]
Transferring thinking to a tool. Useful in small doses. Structural when it becomes the default.

### [[Slopification]]
The systematic replacement of human-made content with AI output at scale. The internet filling with text that was generated, not written.
