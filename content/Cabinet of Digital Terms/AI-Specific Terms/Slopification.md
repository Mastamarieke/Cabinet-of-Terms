# Slopification


***The systematic replacement of human-made content with AI output at scale. The internet filling with text that was generated, not written.***

**Literal meaning:** **Slopification** describes the process by which AI-generated content — produced quickly, cheaply, and at scale — displaces human-made content across information environments. The term "slop" (low-quality AI output) was popularised by Simon Willison in 2024; **slopification** names the structural process by which slop accumulates.

**Origin:** The term entered analytical circulation in 2024 as researchers and commentators documented the rapid increase in AI-generated content across search results, social media, news aggregators, and professional communications. Simon Willison's framing of "**AI slop**" as a category — content that is identifiable as low-effort AI output — was widely adopted. **Slopification** extends this to describe the systemic effect: not just individual pieces of slop but the transformation of information environments by their accumulation.

> The large-scale displacement of human-made content by AI-generated output — degrading the information environment through volume rather than intent.

**The Appeal:** AI-generated content is fast, cheap, and scalable. For organisations under pressure to produce content continuously, it solves a real resource problem. For individuals, it reduces friction on tasks that feel low-stakes. The individual decision to use AI for a blog post or product description is entirely rational.

**The Friction:** The aggregate effect is different from the individual decision. [[AI Hallucination]] — fluently false output — propagates at the same scale as the content itself. [[Synthetic Generation]] — AI-generated content at scale — is the mechanism; **slopification** is the environmental outcome. [[Age of Average]] — the algorithmic homogenisation of aesthetics and ideas — describes what **slopification** produces culturally: a flattening, a narrowing, a convergence on the median. The information environment that AI systems are trained on is increasingly composed of AI-generated content — a feedback loop with no obvious floor.

**Why This Matters:** **Slopification** is the term that names what happens to the commons. Individual AI use is a personal decision. **Slopification** is what that decision looks like at scale, across millions of users, over time.
