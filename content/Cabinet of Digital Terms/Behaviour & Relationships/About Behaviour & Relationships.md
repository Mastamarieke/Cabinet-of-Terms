***Ghosting, phubbing, doomscrolling — these words did not exist thirty years ago. The behaviour they describe mostly did not either.***

This cluster maps the new social patterns that emerged from platform architecture. Many of these behaviours feel personal. They are also structural.

The question this cluster asks: which of your habits were designed for you?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [[Cognitive Surrender]]
You stop looking things up yourself. You stop forming opinions before checking. The thinking gets outsourced — gradually, invisibly.

### [[Phantom Vibration Syndrome]]
Your phone is not vibrating. You feel it anyway. Your nervous system has been recalibrated by a device.

### [[Phatic Interaction]]
"How are you?" "Fine." Nobody meant either sentence. The exchange happened anyway. This is what most digital communication is.

### [[Doomscrolling]]
You know the news is bad. You keep scrolling. The platform is not making you — it just made stopping harder than continuing.

### [[Ghosting]]
No explanation. No ending. Just absence. A behaviour that platform architecture made easier than the conversation.

### [[Phubbing]]
Someone is talking to you. You are looking at your phone. Both of you pretend it is not happening.

### [[Catfishing]]
A false identity, built carefully over time. The platform makes it possible. The loneliness on both sides makes it happen.

### [[Breadcrumbing]]
Enough contact to keep someone interested. Not enough to mean anything. The minimum viable relationship.

### [[Orbiting]]
You ended the relationship. You still watch every story. They know you are there.

### [[Lurker]]
Present but invisible. Reading without responding. Most people on most platforms, most of the time.

### [[Smombie]]
Walking while staring at a screen. A word that needed inventing because the behaviour needed naming.

### [[JOMO]]
The Joy of Missing Out. Marketed as liberation from platforms — on platforms.

### [[Flexing]]
Showing what you have so others know where you stand. Status display, optimised for the feed.
