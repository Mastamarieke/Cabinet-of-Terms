***The watch, the holiday, the car — photographed at the right angle, posted at the right time.***

**Literal meaning:** **Flexing** — from the physical act of **flexing** a muscle to display strength — describes the online display of possessions, achievements, physical appearance, or social connections to signal status. The term comes from African American vernacular English and entered mainstream digital culture through hip-hop and social media from the early 2010s.

**Origin:** The term has roots in 1990s hip-hop culture, where **flexing** described ostentatious displays of wealth and success. It migrated to social media as platforms provided new infrastructure for status display: **Instagram** posts of designer goods, luxury travel, expensive meals, and athletic bodies. Academic work on conspicuous consumption — Thorstein Veblen's _Theory of the Leisure Class_ (1899) — describes the same impulse in a different medium. What Veblen observed among the nineteenth-century leisure class, Rosida (2024) argues, now unfolds on social media as "**flexing** culture": a virtual arena where displaying luxury brands and services serves as a strategy for social recognition and hierarchical differentiation. The mechanism driving this is well documented — exposure to status displays on social media triggers social comparison and fear of missing out, which in turn fuels conspicuous consumption (Dinh & Lee, 2024). In other words, the platform doesn't just host the behavior; it amplifies it.

> The display of status markers — wealth, body, achievement — optimised for social media audiences.

**The Appeal:** Status display is a normal feature of human social life. Sharing achievements, documenting experiences, and showing material success are forms of communication that predate social media. On platforms, **flexing** can build community, attract collaborators, and signal credibility. The impulse is not manufactured by the platform — it is amplified.

**The Friction:** The platform changes the economics of the display. [[Comparison Culture]] — permanent ambient comparison as a platform feature — is what **flexing** feeds: every display is simultaneously a benchmark for everyone who sees it. [[Highlight Reel]] is the format: the flex shows the peak, not the context. [[Attention Economy]] is the infrastructure: the flex performs well algorithmically because status and aspiration drive engagement. The display that was once a local social signal becomes a broadcast — and the social cost of not displaying escalates as everyone else does.

**Why This Matters:** **Flexing** makes visible the feedback loop between status display and platform economics. The platform profits from aspiration. Aspiration requires visible standards. **Flexing** sets those standards. The cycle is self-sustaining.
