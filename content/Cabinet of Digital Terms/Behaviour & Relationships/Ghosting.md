***No explanation. No ending. Just absence.***

**Literal meaning:** **Ghosting** describes ending a relationship — romantic, professional, or social — by ceasing all communication without explanation. The person simply stops responding. The other party receives no closure, no reason, and no acknowledgement that the relationship has ended.

**Origin:** The term entered common use around 2014–2015, coinciding with the widespread adoption of smartphone-based dating apps. It is not a new behaviour — people have always avoided difficult conversations — but the term named it specifically in the context of digital communication, where the infrastructure for both maintaining and ending contact is the same platform. LeFebvre et al. (2019) frame **ghosting** as a "digital dissolution disappearance strategy": a technologically enabled form of relationship termination that relies on the very affordances that made the connection possible — the ease of mediated contact also makes silence effortless. The choice to ghost is not random, however. Freedman et al. (2019) found that individuals who hold stronger "destiny beliefs" — the implicit theory that relationships are either meant to be or not — are more likely to view **ghosting** as acceptable and to employ it themselves. In other words, the platform provides the means, but underlying beliefs about relationships shape who uses it.

> Ending a relationship through sustained non-response — a behaviour that digital infrastructure made easier than the conversation it replaces.

**The Appeal:** **Ghosting** avoids conflict. For the person doing it, it sidesteps an uncomfortable conversation with someone they may never see again. The platform enables it seamlessly: you simply stop. No formal ending is required because no formal beginning was required either. In the context of app-mediated connection at scale, some form of attrition is inevitable.

**The Friction:** The asymmetry is the problem. For the person ghosted, the absence provides no information — leaving the relationship in a state of unresolved ambiguity that research associates with prolonged psychological distress. [[Breadcrumbing]] — maintaining just enough contact to keep someone interested — is the active form of the same dynamic. [[Orbiting]] is **ghosting** with continued passive observation. What all three share is the use of platform features to manage relational distance without taking relational responsibility. The platform made it possible. The platform made it normal.

**Why This Matters:** **Ghosting** makes visible what platform architecture does to the social norms around endings. The absence of friction in digital communication removes the friction that makes difficult conversations happen.
