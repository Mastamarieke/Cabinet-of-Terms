# Hypergamy


***The claim: women always want someone higher on the hierarchy. The evidence: personal anecdote, presented as evolutionary biology.***

**Literal meaning:** **Hypergamy** — from Greek *hyper* (above) and *gamos* (marriage) — in its sociological sense describes the practice of marrying someone of higher social status. In **manosphere** discourse, it is redeployed as a universal female biological drive: women are hardwired to seek partners with higher status, resources, and dominance than themselves, and to continuously seek upgrades. The claim is presented as evolutionary biology.

**Origin:** The sociological concept of **hypergamy** has been used in anthropology and sociology to describe marriage patterns, particularly in caste societies. The **manosphere** appropriation strips the historical and cultural specificity from the concept and presents it as a universal biological law. The claim draws selectively on evolutionary psychology — specifically sexual selection theory — while ignoring the substantial research complicating or contradicting it.

> A sociological term describing marriage patterns, captured and redeployed as a universal biological law to explain men's romantic failures.

**The Appeal:** **Hypergamy** provides an explanation for rejection that locates the cause externally: it is not that I am unattractive or unsuitable, it is that women are biologically programmed to reject men who are not high enough status. It also generates a prescription: become high status, and the biological drive will work in your favour. The evolutionary framing gives the claim scientific authority.

**The Friction:** The scientific basis is contested. Evolutionary psychology research on mate preferences is substantially more complex, contextually variable, and contested than the **manosphere** **hypergamy** claim implies. The claim is also unfalsifiable as deployed: any relationship where a woman dates a man of higher status confirms it; any relationship where she dates a man of lower status is explained away. [[SMV (Sexual Market Value)]] is the market framework that makes **hypergamy** operational: if attractiveness is a score, **hypergamy** describes how women shop. [[Womanosphere]] adopts the same concept in a different register: women should leverage their hypergamic drive strategically. [[Incel]] ideology uses **hypergamy** to explain why men at the bottom of the SMV hierarchy are rejected: they are simply not high enough value to overcome female hypergamic selection.

**Why This Matters:** **Hypergamy** makes visible how scientific-sounding vocabulary can function as an unfalsifiable explanation for personal experience. Once the claim is biological and universal, no counter-evidence is possible. That's not science — it's a closed loop.
