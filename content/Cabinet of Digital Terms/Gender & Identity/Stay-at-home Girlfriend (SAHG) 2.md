# Stay-at-home Girlfriend (SAHG)


***Financial dependence without marriage. A relationship model promoted as freedom — on platforms built by investors.***

**Literal meaning:** A stay-at-home girlfriend is a woman who does not work for pay, instead performing domestic labour — cooking, cleaning, homemaking — within an unmarried cohabiting relationship financially supported by her partner. The model is explicitly distinguished from the **tradwife** by the absence of marriage and, often, children.

**Origin:** The term gained **TikTok** and **Instagram** visibility from around 2022, associated with content showing aestheticised domestic life — morning routines, cooking, homemaking — framed as a lifestyle choice and relationship dynamic. The content positions the SAHG as liberated from workplace stress and feminine **hustle culture**, supported by a **provider** partner.

> Financial dependence without marriage — a relationship model that performs liberation from professional identity while reproducing economic dependency.

**The Appeal:** The SAHG framework addresses genuine exhaustion with the pressure on women to maintain both career and domestic life. The content promises an alternative: be supported, focus on homemaking, opt out of the workforce grind. For women in relationships where this is financially viable, it may function as described.

**The Friction:** The economic vulnerability is the structural problem the aesthetic conceals. Unlike marriage, which in most jurisdictions provides legal protections for the economically dependent partner, the SAHG relationship has no such framework. [[Provider]] — the male caretaker role as transaction — is the complementary position: the dynamic is identical to tradwife but without the legal and religious structures that historically accompanied it. [[Tradwife]] at least carries the security of marriage; SAHG does not. [[SMV (Sexual Market Value)]] logic underlies both: the woman's domestic contribution is her market offering in exchange for financial support. [[Womanosphere]] distributes the framework as empowerment content, typically without discussing the legal and economic risks.

**Why This Matters:** SAHG makes visible the gap between lifestyle content and structural analysis. The aesthetic is about freedom; the economics are about dependency. Once you see the legal framework absent from the content, the "freedom" becomes a different kind of sentence.
