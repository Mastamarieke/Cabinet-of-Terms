***They walk the same route every day, say the same things, react the same way. The word borrowed from gaming to describe people.***

**Literal meaning:** NPC — Non-Player Character — is a gaming term for a character in a video game controlled by the computer rather than a human player. NPCs perform scripted behaviours, repeat the same actions, and lack genuine agency or inner life. Applied to real people, it describes someone seen as going through the motions without real thought, original opinion, or autonomous will.

**Origin:** The gaming term migrated into political and social discourse around 2016–2018, when it became a meme format in alt-right and red-pill communities to describe people who hold mainstream political views — implying they are not capable of genuine thought but simply repeat programmed responses. The NPC meme spread widely on 4chan and Twitter before being restricted on some platforms for dehumanising content.

> A gaming term for a character without autonomous will, applied to real people to deny their inner life — dehumanisation borrowed from entertainment.

**The Appeal:** The NPC frame offers a satisfying explanation for why people hold views you find incomprehensible: they are not reasoning agents who reached different conclusions, they are NPCs executing programming. This removes the cognitive burden of engaging with opposing views while providing a sense of epistemological superiority. The red-pill user sees; the NPC does not.

**The Friction:** [[Dehumanization]] — the denial of full humanity to a person or group — is the mechanism. The NPC label is lighter than "**subhuman**" but functions structurally similarly: it denies the target genuine interiority, making dismissal rather than engagement the appropriate response. [[Gamification Creep]] — game logic applied to non-game contexts — describes the mechanism: the vocabulary of video games is applied to political reality, with predictable consequences for how political opponents are perceived. [[Sigma Male]] shares the structure: both concepts position the self as the uniquely conscious observer surrounded by automatons. [[Red Pill]] epistemology — the world divided into those who see and those who are programmed — is the framework that makes NPC logic coherent.

**Why This Matters:** NPC makes visible how gaming vocabulary can carry dehumanising logic into political discourse without triggering the recognition that more explicit dehumanising language would produce. The game frame provides cover.
