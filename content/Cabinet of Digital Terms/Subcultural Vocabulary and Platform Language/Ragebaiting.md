# Ragebaiting


***The post was obviously wrong. Deliberately wrong. You commented to correct it.***

**Literal meaning:** **Ragebaiting** describes content — posts, headlines, videos, statements — deliberately designed to provoke anger, outrage, or indignation in the audience, exploiting the algorithmic fact that angry engagement (replies, shares, quote-posts, comments) generates more distribution than neutral or positive engagement.

**Origin:** The term was selected by Oxford University Press as Word of the Year 2025, reflecting its prevalence in describing a documented content strategy. The underlying mechanism was identified earlier: Facebook's own internal research (later published by journalists including Jeff Horwitz and cited in the Frances Haugen disclosures) documented that content triggering "angry" emoji reactions received algorithmic amplification — creating an incentive structure that rewards anger-producing content.

> Content engineered to produce outrage — where the emotional response is the distribution mechanism and the platform profits from both.

**The Appeal:** **Ragebaiting** works, which is its appeal to producers. Content that makes people angry gets shared, debated, and responded to at rates that neutral content does not. For creators, journalists, and politicians, the engagement metrics are real and have real consequences for reach and revenue. The audience's anger is also genuinely engaged — it is not passive, and it feels important.

**The Friction:** [[Attention Economy]] — attention as scarce commodity — is the structural driver: anger is the most efficiently harvested emotion because it produces the most engagement per unit of content. [[Recommender Systems]] are the distribution mechanism: content that produces high engagement is shown to more people, regardless of its accuracy or social value.  and  are the rhetorical tools: the outrage-producing representation of the opposing view is almost always a weakened caricature. [[Flood the Zone]] is the strategic application at scale. The term's selection as Word of the Year 2025 marks the cultural moment at which the practice became widely named and recognised.

**Why This Matters:** **Ragebaiting** names the mechanism that converts anger into distribution. Once you see it, every piece of content that makes you angry becomes a question: is this anger a natural response to a real injustice, or is the anger the product being manufactured? The two are not mutually exclusive — but the distinction matters.
