# Cooked


***One word. Absolute certainty. No appeal.***

**Literal meaning:** "**Cooked**" is internet slang for a state of irreversible failure, defeat, or ruin. A person, situation, argument, or plan that is "**cooked**" is finished — beyond recovery, exposed, or definitively defeated. The culinary metaphor implies completion: what is **cooked** cannot be uncooked.

**Origin:** The term circulates in Black internet culture and gaming communities, entering broader internet slang from around 2020–2022. It functions as a one-word verdict: concise, final, and often delivered with dark humour. Its usage spans individual situations ("that argument is **cooked**"), interpersonal conflicts ("he's **cooked** after that ratio"), and civilisational assessments ("we're all **cooked**" — adjacent to doomer language).

> A one-word verdict of irreversible failure — applied across registers from competitive gaming to personal crisis, with the same tone of absolute finality.

**The Appeal:** "**Cooked**" is rhetorically efficient. It compresses judgment into one word, implies shared understanding of the criteria, and performs confident assessment with minimal elaboration. In a media environment that rewards quick, decisive takes, "**cooked**" does the work of a paragraph in one syllable.

**The Friction:** The finality of "**cooked**" is also its analytical problem.  — engaging with the strongest version of an opposing position — is incompatible with "**cooked**": the verdict forecloses the engagement. In political and social discourse, "**cooked**" functions as a crowd verdict that does not require justification. [[Doomerism]] and "**cooked**" share a structural family resemblance at the civilisational scale: both present an irreversible negative outcome as a given. [[Ratio'd]] is one mechanism by which someone becomes "**cooked**": the public metric confirms the verdict. The humour that typically accompanies "**cooked**" is a register that enables the finality: delivered ironically, it is both serious and deniable.

**Why This Matters:** **Cooked** makes visible how one-word verdicts function in internet discourse. Once you see the finality as a rhetorical choice — not a fact — you can ask: by whose criteria, and is the reversibility actually as fixed as the word implies?
