# Subcultural Vocabulary and Platform Language


***Language that starts online, moves offline, and mutates constantly. By the time you know what it means, it already means something else.***

This cluster maps the mechanisms by which platform architecture shapes language — and how that language travels from screens into everyday speech, carrying its original context with it. Some of this vocabulary is playful. Some of it is ideological. Often it is both simultaneously, which is part of what makes it analytically interesting.

The decontextualisation is the story: words that originated in specific communities — gaming, incel forums, Black American internet culture — spread through algorithmic amplification into general use, stripped of their original meaning. The spread is not neutral. The context travels invisibly with the word.

The question this cluster asks: where did this word come from, and what did it mean when it got there?

## Terms in this cluster

### [[Algospeak]]
Words modified to survive content moderation. "Unalive" instead of "die." Language adapting to the algorithm in real time.

### [[Aurafarming]]
Quantifying social status as a game. Accumulating presence and perceived value — without saying anything directly.

### [[Coded -Pilled]]
Productive suffixes that mark identity and signal ideological conversion simultaneously. The efficiency of the vocabulary is inseparable from its resistance to interrogation.

### [[Brain Rot]]
Oxford Word of the Year 2024. Subcultural self-diagnosis for the cognitive flatness that follows extended algorithmic consumption.

### [[Chad]]
The aspirational apex of manosphere identity — the naturally dominant male against whom all men are ranked. Migrated into general internet use as ironic praise, obscuring its ideological origins. *(new V10; primary; dual: Gender & Identity)*

### [[Cooked]]
Irreversibly lost. A definitive verdict in one word. No appeal.

### [[Crash-out]]
A public loss of control as a performative moment. The line between genuine distress and performed content is structurally blurred.

### [[Gooner]]
Compulsive fixation as public vocabulary. A word that originated in explicit subculture and migrated into general use through platform spread.

### [[IRL (In Real Life)]]
Physical reality announcing itself to distinguish from the digital default. The term that reveals which mode of existence is now unmarked. *(dual primary: Counter-Movements)*

### [[Main Character Energy]]
Framing yourself as the protagonist of your own story. Everyone else as supporting cast or obstacle.

### [[Podcast-bro]]
The male podcaster as ideological gatekeeper. Long-form, unedited, enormously influential.

### [[Ragebaiting]]
Content designed to provoke anger. Anger travels faster than any other emotion.

### [[Ratio'd]]
More replies than likes. Public punishment via platform metric.

### [[Tierlists S-Tier]]
Ranking everything — characters, fast food, people — on a scale from S to F. Game logic applied to the world.
