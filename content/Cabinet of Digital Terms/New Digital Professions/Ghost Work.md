# Ghost Work

***The platform runs on human labour. The interface shows none of it.***

**Literal meaning**
*Ghost* — invisible, absent from the record. *Work* — labour. **Ghost Work** is the term Mary Gray and Siddharth Suri coined for the invisible human labour that underpins digital platforms and AI systems.

**Origin**
Mary Gray and Siddharth Suri coined the term in *Ghost Work* (2019), documenting the global ecosystem of on-demand workers who perform the tasks that automation cannot — content moderation, data labelling, quality control — through platforms including Amazon MTurk, CrowdFlower, and Appen. Casilli (2024) extends the analysis to AI training pipelines, arguing that **ghost work** is structurally embedded in the production of all contemporary AI.

> Invisible on-demand human labour that makes digital platforms and AI systems function — essential to the operation, erased from the product narrative.

**The Appeal**
**Ghost work** provides income and flexibility to workers globally, many in regions without equivalent formal employment alternatives.

**The Friction**
Gray and Suri document the structural precarity: no minimum wage, no benefits, no continuity, no appeal against algorithmic dismissal. The labour is invisible by design — platforms present their products as automated precisely because the human labour undermines the narrative of technological autonomy. [[Mechanical Turk]] is the emblematic platform; **ghost work** names the broader condition.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see **ghost work** as the hidden foundation of AI, you see that every interaction with an "intelligent" system rests on a supply chain of invisible, poorly protected human labour — and that the invisibility is a product decision.
