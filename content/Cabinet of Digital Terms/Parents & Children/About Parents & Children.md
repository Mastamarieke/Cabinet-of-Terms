# Parents & Children


***Children did not ask to be born into the attention economy. But they were.***

This cluster maps the specific ways platforms exploit the vulnerabilities of minors — and the ways parental behaviour sometimes reproduces that same logic without meaning to. The risks are not accidental. They are built in.

The question this cluster asks: who is responsible for the child in the feed?

---

## Terms in this cluster

### [[Sharenting]]
Before the child can speak, they have a digital footprint. Someone else built it.

### [[Momfluencer]]
Mother as content producer. Child as protagonist. The domestic sphere as a business model.

### [[Family Vlogging]]
The camera is always on. The children are always in frame. They did not audition.

### [[Kidfluencer]]
A child with a brand, managed by a parent. An industry with no minimum age requirement.

### [[Screen Time]]
The paediatrician asks how many hours. The app tracks it. The platform that designed the product to be irresistible is not part of the conversation.

### [[Roblox]]
It looks like a game. It is also a currency system, a social network, and a space where adults access children.

### [[Robux]]
The child does not spend money. They spend Robux. The money was already spent.

### [[Loot Boxes]]
You pay. You do not know what you get. The same mechanism drives slot machines. There is no age requirement.

### [[Predatory Design]]
The game knows the child is seven. It knows which notifications bring them back and how long they will play before a parent intervenes. This was designed in.

### [[Gaming Disorder]]
The WHO gave it a name. The gaming industry said it did not exist. The child could not stop playing.

### [[Online Grooming]]
The conversation begins as friendship. It moves gradually. Each step is small enough to seem unremarkable.

### [[Brain Rot]]
You open the app. Forty minutes pass. Something feels different — slower, flatter. You already know what it is called.

### [[FOMO]]
The phone lights up. You do not check it immediately. But you wonder what you are missing. That wondering is the product.

### [[Parasocial Relationship]]
The child talks about the YouTuber as if they know them. They feel they do. The YouTuber does not know the child exists.

### [[Sexting]]
The legal framework was written for adults. The behaviour it criminalises is common among teenagers.
