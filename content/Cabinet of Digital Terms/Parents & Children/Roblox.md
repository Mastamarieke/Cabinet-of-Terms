# Roblox


***It looks like a game. It has its own currency. It has its own economy. It is designed for children.***

**Literal meaning:** **Roblox** is a gaming platform that allows users to create and play games built by other users within its environment. With over 200 million monthly active users, the majority under 16, it is one of the world's most popular platforms for children. It is also a currency system (**Robux**), a social network (chat, friend requests, direct messaging), and a commercial ecosystem.

**Origin:** **Roblox** Corporation was founded in 2004 and launched publicly in 2006. The platform grew substantially during the COVID-19 pandemic — by 2021, **Roblox** reported that children in the US aged 9–12 spent more time on the platform on average than watching television. The platform's IPO in 2021 valued it at approximately $38 billion.

> A children's platform whose name reveals nothing about what it is — simultaneously a game engine, a social network, an economy, and a data collection operation.

**The Appeal:** **Roblox** provides genuine creative and social value. Children build games, collaborate with friends, and develop basic programming and design skills. The user-generated content model means that the platform is constantly new. For many children, **Roblox** is their primary social space.

**The Friction:** The platform's name conceals its complexity. [[Robux]] — the virtual currency — introduces real-money transaction logic to children as young as seven. The social features — chat, friend requests, direct messaging — are the same features that make [[Online Grooming]] possible: adults presenting as children, building trust over time, moving towards exploitation. Multiple documented cases of grooming have occurred on **Roblox**. [[Predatory Design]] — design choices that maximise engagement — is embedded throughout: notification systems, social pressure mechanics, and virtual currency all operate on children's developmental vulnerabilities. The platform's parental controls exist but are non-default and require technical knowledge to configure.

**Why This Matters:** **Roblox** makes visible how a platform name can function as camouflage. "**Roblox**" conveys nothing about currency, social networking, or access risk. Once you know what the platform is — all of it — the parental conversation about "are you playing **Roblox**?" sounds different.
