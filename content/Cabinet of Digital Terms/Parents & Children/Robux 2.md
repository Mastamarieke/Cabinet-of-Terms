# Robux


***The money is not called money. It is called Robux. The child does not notice the difference.***

**Literal meaning:** **Robux** is the virtual currency of the **Roblox** platform. Real money is converted into **Robux** to purchase virtual items — avatar accessories, game passes, and in-game content. The exchange rate converts the psychological reality of money into an abstracted number, reducing the friction of spending.

**Origin:** **Robux** was introduced as **Roblox**'s currency system from the platform's early years. The mechanism of converting real money into platform-specific virtual currency before purchasing is documented in Gray et al.'s (2024) **dark patterns** ontology as "Intermediate Currency" — a meso-level dark pattern under the Obstruction strategy: it hides the true cost by requiring real money to be converted into virtual currency first.

> A virtual currency that converts real money into an abstracted number — reducing the psychological weight of spending for children who cannot yet evaluate the exchange.

**The Appeal:** Virtual currencies simplify transactions in game economies and are a legitimate feature of many platforms. For **Roblox**, **Robux** allows a marketplace of user-created items. For children, the in-game economy is part of the social experience of the platform.

**The Friction:** The conversion serves the platform's interests. A child spending "400 **Robux**" is spending approximately €4.50, but the psychological experience of spending a number is different from spending money. [[Predatory Design]] — design choices that exploit children's developmental vulnerabilities — includes this mechanism precisely: the cognitive capacity to evaluate monetary value develops late in childhood, and virtual currencies exploit that developmental gap. [[Loot Boxes]] extend the same logic into probabilistic spending. The UK Gambling Commission and the Belgian Gaming Commission have both investigated whether virtual currency systems in games constitute gambling; regulatory positions vary.

**Why This Matters:** **Robux** makes visible the specific mechanism by which real economic transactions are obscured from children. The number is real. The money it represents is less visible. Once you see the conversion as a design choice, the "it's just **Robux**" response to a child's spending becomes a different sentence.
