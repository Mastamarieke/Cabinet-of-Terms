# Digital Exclusion

The benefit claim form is online only. The pensioner does not have internet. This is described as a technical limitation. It is a policy choice.

**Literal meaning**
*Digital* — relating to digital technology. *Exclusion* — being left out, denied access or participation. Digital Exclusion describes being unable to participate in digital society — through design, cost, skills, or policy.

**Origin**
Digital Exclusion emerged as a concept in the 2000s as public services moved online. Research from the Good Things Foundation, Age UK, and others documented the consequences for people who cannot access digital-only services — including inability to claim benefits, access healthcare information, or participate in employment.

> Digital Exclusion is the active exclusion of people from digital participation — through design that does not accommodate them, policies that assume connectivity, or costs that prevent access.

**The Appeal**
Identifying Digital Exclusion as a distinct category enables targeted policy responses: specific programmes for specific excluded groups.

**The Friction**
[[public/inclusion_ageing/digital_divide_public|Digital Divide]] describes the gap; Digital Exclusion names the process that produces it — and the active role of design and policy in maintaining it. [[public/platform_mechanisms/dark_patterns_public|Dark Patterns]] contribute: interfaces designed for engaged, skilled users actively exclude those who are not.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Digital Exclusion as an active process rather than a passive gap, you see that every design decision and every policy choice about digital access is also a decision about who is included and who is not.
