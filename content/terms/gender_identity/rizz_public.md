# Rizz

You are trying to talk to someone, and it feels like everyone else has a script you were never given.

**Literal meaning**
*Rizz* — short for charisma — a slang term that compresses social charm and flirting ability into a trait you either have or can acquire.

**Origin**
Popularised by streamer Kai Cenat around 2021, the term spread from gaming communities to TikTok and mainstream youth culture. Oxford named it one of the words of the year in 2023. What began as subcultural shorthand became a searchable skill — tutorials, ratings, tier lists.

> Rizz is charisma repackaged as a performance metric — a skill that can be learned, optimised, and ranked.

**The Appeal**
The term does something useful for people who feel socially anxious: it externalises the problem. If you lack rizz, the issue is technical, not fundamental. You can study it, practice it, improve it. [[terms/gender_identity/smv_public|SMV]] — Sexual Market Value, the idea that attractiveness can be scored — gives you a benchmark. [[terms/subcultural_language/aurafarming_public|Aurafarming]] — quantifying social status as a game, accumulating presence and coolness as if levelling up — is the logical extension: rizz is not just a trait but a resource to farm. That is genuinely appealing when rejection feels devastating and personal.

**The Friction**
But the metric that promises control also reframes every interaction as a performance under judgment. The same platforms selling the skill sell the anxiety that makes you need it. Tutorials on rizz route toward [[terms/gender_identity/looksmaxxing_public|Looksmaxxing]] — optimising appearance as an identity project — and from there toward [[terms/gender_identity/blackpill_public|Blackpill]] determinism, where charm is framed as genetic fate rather than learned behaviour. **Recommender Systems** amplify the pipeline because anxious users engage more. Someone like Kai Cenat built an audience of millions on exactly this tension — the charm that looks effortless is the one most relentlessly performed.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see rizz as a platform product rather than a personal quality, you see that the skill being sold is not social confidence — it is continued engagement with content about social confidence. The anxiety and the remedy are the same product.
