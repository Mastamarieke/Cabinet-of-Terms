# Lurker

You have been reading this forum for three years. You have never posted. You know everything about the regulars. They do not know you exist.

**Literal meaning**
*Lurk* — to remain hidden while watching. A lurker is someone who participates in an online community by consuming content without contributing or identifying themselves.

**Origin**
The term predates social media — it was used in early internet forums and Usenet groups from the 1980s onward. Lurking has always been the majority behaviour in online communities: research consistently shows that a small fraction of users produce most content, while the majority consume silently.

> Lurking is the practice of consuming online community content without participating — the majority behaviour in most platforms, invisible to metrics but significant in aggregate.

**The Appeal**
Consuming without contributing is low-risk: no embarrassment, no conflict, no commitment. For people in new communities, lurking is how you learn the norms before participating.

**The Friction**
Platforms count lurkers as engagement — views, impressions, time-on-site — while capturing their data without their active participation. [[terms/platform_mechanisms/recommender_systems_public|Recommender Systems]] build behavioural profiles from lurking data as effectively as from active engagement. The lurker believes they are watching without being watched.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see lurking as a data-generating activity rather than passive non-participation, you see that the choice not to post does not mean the choice not to contribute to platform economics.
