# Breadcrumbing

An occasional like. A message once a week. Just enough to keep you interested. Not enough to mean anything.

**Literal meaning**
From the fairy tale: breadcrumbs left as a trail to follow. Breadcrumbing is leaving minimal traces of interest — just enough to maintain another person's attention and emotional investment without commitment.

**Origin**
The term emerged in dating culture discourse around 2017, as dating apps made it easy to maintain multiple partial connections simultaneously. It names a behaviour enabled by low-cost digital contact: you can sustain someone's interest with minimal effort because the platform makes minimal effort possible.

> Breadcrumbing is the deliberate maintenance of minimal contact to sustain another person's interest and emotional investment without genuine commitment.

**The Appeal**
Keeping options open is a rational strategy in a platform environment that continuously surfaces new alternatives. Breadcrumbing preserves a connection at minimal cost.

**The Friction**
[[terms/platform_mechanisms/dopamine_feedback_loops_public|Dopamine Feedback Loops]] — variable reward schedules — operate at the relational level: intermittent attention is more compelling than consistent attention, and the breadcrumber exploits this. The platform architecture that enables low-cost contact also makes it easy to be a source of intermittent reward for someone else.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see breadcrumbing as a relational pattern shaped by platform economics rather than individual cruelty, you see that the cost structure of digital contact has changed what counts as maintaining a relationship.
