# Online Grooming

The conversation begins as friendship. It moves gradually. Each step is small enough to seem unremarkable. The child does not know it is a process. That is the process.

**Literal meaning:** Online grooming is the deliberate process by which an adult establishes an emotional relationship with a child through digital platforms, with the intent of facilitating sexual abuse or exploitation. Grooming involves building trust, normalising increasingly intimate or inappropriate communication, isolating the child from trusted adults, and creating conditions for abuse. It occurs across all platforms where children are present, but is documented with particular frequency on gaming platforms, social media, and messaging apps.

**Origin:** The term entered legal and child protection frameworks in the early 2000s, as digital communication created new contexts for adult-child contact outside parental oversight. The UK Sexual Offences Act 2003 introduced specific offences for grooming; similar legislation followed in other jurisdictions. The term has since become central to child safeguarding training, platform policy, and public health discourse. Its appearance in relation to Roblox and similar platforms reflects the structural access those platforms provide: chat features, private messaging, and avatar-based interaction create conditions that groomers exploit systematically.

> The deliberate process of building trust with a child through digital platforms in order to facilitate sexual abuse — exploiting the same design features that make platforms engaging for children.

**The Appeal:** There is no appeal to online grooming as a practice — the entry addresses it from the perspective of understanding the mechanism, not validating it. What requires analysis is why it persists at scale: the platform features that enable grooming — direct messaging, anonymous or pseudonymous interaction, social features designed for connection — are the same features that make platforms valuable for children's legitimate social lives. Removing them entirely would destroy the product; regulating them requires enforcement capacity that most platforms do not maintain.

**The Friction:** [[Predatory Design]] — design maximising engagement — is not designed to enable grooming, but creates the conditions it exploits. [[Roblox]] is repeatedly cited in grooming cases because its social features, large child user base, and partial anonymity create structural access. [[Parasocial Relationship]] — one-sided emotional bonds with perceived known individuals — describes the psychological mechanism groomers exploit and manufacture: the child believes they have a genuine friendship with someone who has constructed the relationship instrumentally. [[Sharenting]] and [[Family Vlogging]] contribute indirectly: detailed, routine, geotagged documentation of a child's life provides groomers with information that accelerates trust-building. Platform design is not the cause of grooming — abusers are — but it is the infrastructure that determines its scale.

**Why This Matters:** Online grooming makes visible the responsibility gap between platform design and child safety. Platforms create social infrastructure for children without designing it with their protection as a primary value. The features that enable harm are features, not bugs.
