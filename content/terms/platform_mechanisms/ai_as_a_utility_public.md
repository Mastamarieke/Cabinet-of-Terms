# AI as a Utility

**Is AI becoming the new electricity — or a creeping monopoly you never see but always pay for?**

---

## Literal Meaning

- **AI:** Artificial Intelligence — machines that simulate thinking.
- **Utility:** A public service like water or electricity — always available, invisible infrastructure, paid via subscription or taxes.

---

## Origin

The framing went mainstream around 2023–2024, pushed by tech leaders like **Satya Nadella** (Microsoft) and **Sam Altman** (OpenAI). Their pitch: AI should flow from the wall socket — no need to understand it, just use it. By 2025, this narrative had shifted from "revolution" to "infrastructure," hiding the centralized control behind a promise of democratization.

---

## Definition

> "AI as a utility promises democratic access but hides a centrally controlled infrastructure no user has any say over."

---

## The Friction

Once AI becomes a "utility," choice disappears. You can't switch providers when your model goes down. Costs are buried in subscriptions or data extraction. The real price isn't money — it's **dependency**: when the tap shuts off, your work, creativity, and communication stop. What's hidden is that there is no public infrastructure here — just private ownership by a handful of companies.

---

## Alternatives

- Open-source models running locally.
- Public AI infrastructure (think: an "AI water board").
- Transparent per-query cost accounting.
