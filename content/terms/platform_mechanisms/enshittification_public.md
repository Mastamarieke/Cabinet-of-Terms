# Enshittification

The platform used to be good. Then it changed. Now it is worse. This happens to every platform. There is a name for it.

**Literal meaning**
Coined by Cory Doctorow in 2022. *En-* — causative prefix. *Shit* — degraded quality. *-ification* — process of becoming. Enshittification is the process by which platforms structurally deteriorate for users.

**Origin**
Cory Doctorow introduced the term in 2022 to describe the documented lifecycle of platforms: first good for users, then good for business customers, then good only for shareholders — extracting maximum value before users have no viable exit.

> Enshittification is the structural process by which platforms degrade their user experience over time — extracting increasing value from users as switching costs make exit difficult.

**The Appeal**
Platforms are genuinely good in their early phases — they need users before they can monetise them.

**The Friction**
[[public/privacy_compliance/vendor_lock_in_public|Vendor Lock-in]] — the condition where switching costs prevent exit — is what makes enshittification possible. Once users, data, and social networks are locked in, the platform can degrade without losing the audience. [[public/society_power/technofeudalism_public|Technofeudalism]] — platform owners extracting rent from those who cannot leave — is the structural context.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see enshittification as a predictable cycle rather than a series of bad decisions, you see that platform decline is not accidental — it is the business model working as intended.
