# Algospeak

You cannot say the word. So you spell it wrong. Or replace one letter. Or use an emoji. The algorithm does not notice. The audience does.

**Literal meaning**
*Algo* — algorithm. *Speak* — language. Algospeak is the vocabulary of deliberate linguistic modification to evade automated content moderation.

**Origin**
Algospeak emerged on TikTok and spread across platforms as content moderation became more automated. Users developed substitutes for flagged terms: "unalive" for suicide, "seggs" for sex, "le dollar bean" for the disability emoji. The practice became a documented linguistic phenomenon studied by internet linguists.

> Algospeak is the deliberate modification of language to evade algorithmic content moderation — a living vocabulary that evolves faster than the systems trying to suppress it.

**The Appeal**
Algospeak enables people to discuss difficult topics — mental health, sexuality, political repression — in spaces where those topics are suppressed. It is also a form of subcultural creativity and in-group signalling.

**The Friction**
The same mechanism that protects marginalised speakers also protects harmful content from moderation. [[terms/platform_mechanisms/recommender_systems_public|Recommender Systems]] — optimising for engagement — cannot distinguish between the two without context. Algospeak is a symptom of automated moderation's fundamental limitation: it operates on signals, not meaning.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see algospeak as a linguistic adaptation to algorithmic power rather than mere slang, you see the underlying dynamic: when automated systems control language, language evolves to circumvent automated systems.
