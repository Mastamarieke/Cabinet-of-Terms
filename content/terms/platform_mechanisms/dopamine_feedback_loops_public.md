# Dopamine Feedback Loops

You refresh. Nothing new. You refresh again. A notification. A like. You put the phone down. You pick it up again.

**Literal meaning**
*Dopamine* — neurotransmitter associated with reward and anticipation. *Feedback Loop* — a cycle where outputs become inputs. Dopamine Feedback Loops are design patterns that exploit the neuroscience of reward anticipation to sustain engagement.

**Origin**
The connection between variable reward schedules and compulsive behaviour was established by B.F. Skinner in the 1950s. Social media platforms applied this research — documented by former insiders including Aza Raskin, who invented the infinite scroll, and Tristan Harris — to design systems that maximise return visits.

> Dopamine Feedback Loops are platform design patterns that exploit variable reward schedules — the same mechanism behind gambling — to sustain compulsive engagement.

**The Appeal**
The rewards are real: social validation, interesting content, genuine connection. The loop is not purely manufactured.

**The Friction**
The variable schedule is the key mechanism — rewards that arrive unpredictably are more compelling than predictable ones. [[public/platform_mechanisms/gamification_creep_public|Gamification Creep]] — game logic entering non-game contexts — deploys the same mechanism. The design does not need users to enjoy the experience. It needs them to return.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see dopamine feedback loops as a deliberate design choice rather than an accidental side effect, you stop asking why you cannot stop and start asking who benefits from the answer.
