# Ragebaiting

You knew it was designed to make you angry. You commented anyway.

**Literal meaning**
*Rage* — intense anger. *Baiting* — deliberately provoking a reaction. Ragebaiting is the deliberate production of content designed to provoke anger, because angry engagement drives algorithmic amplification.

**Origin**
Oxford University Press named ragebaiting as a word of the year contender in 2025. The practice predates the term: platforms discovered that anger drives more engagement than other emotions. Content optimised for outrage — through provocative framing, selective information, and tribal activation — systematically outperforms calm, informative content.

> Ragebaiting is the deliberate production of content designed to provoke anger — exploiting the fact that rage drives engagement metrics more effectively than other emotional responses.

**The Appeal**
There is no appeal from the audience perspective — but the anger is often directed at something genuinely worth being angry about. Ragebaiting works by attaching to real grievances.

**The Friction**
[[terms/platform_mechanisms/recommender_systems_public|Recommender Systems]] — optimising for engagement — amplify ragebaiting content because anger produces more comments, shares, and return visits than satisfaction. [[terms/platform_mechanisms/clickbait_public|Clickbait]] uses the same mechanism at the headline level. The outrage is manufactured; the underlying issue is often real; the engagement benefits the platform regardless.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see ragebaiting as a deliberate strategy rather than provocative content, you see that your anger has a commercial value — and that someone is harvesting it.
