# Red Teamer

Their job is to break it — before someone else does.

**Literal meaning:** A red teamer is a professional who attempts to find failures, vulnerabilities, and harmful capabilities in AI systems by systematically trying to elicit outputs the system was designed to prevent: dangerous instructions, biased outputs, privacy violations, manipulation tactics, and more. The term comes from military and security culture, where a "red team" simulates an adversary to test a system's defences. Applied to AI, it describes both formal safety testing within companies and independent adversarial evaluation.

**Origin:** Red teaming as a formal practice in AI development gained public visibility with the release of large language models from 2022 onward, as companies including Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google began publishing information about adversarial testing before model releases. The practice became a condition of some regulatory frameworks and voluntary commitments. Independent red teaming organisations — groups that test models outside the companies that build them — also emerged, operating with varying degrees of access and disclosure.

> A professional who systematically attempts to elicit harmful, false, or unintended outputs from AI systems — finding what the system should not be able to do but can.

**The Appeal:** Red teaming is one of the few professions in AI development explicitly oriented toward finding problems rather than building capabilities. It requires creativity, adversarial thinking, and deep knowledge of how models fail — a combination that is genuinely scarce and genuinely valuable. For people who care about AI safety, it is one of the most direct ways to contribute to it from inside the system.

**The Friction:** The profession's independence is its structural weakness. Red teamers employed by AI companies operate under NDAs, are subject to company disclosure decisions, and may face pressure to underreport findings that delay a release. [[Black Box]] — structural opacity — limits what external red teamers can access and therefore what they can test. [[Obfuscation]] — deliberately maintaining opacity — can extend to red teaming processes themselves: companies can claim adversarial testing without disclosing its scope, methodology, or findings. [[Sycophancy (AI)]] — AI systems that confirm what users want to hear — is a specific failure mode that red teaming targets, but which is also reproduced structurally when red teamers' findings are filtered through the interests of the organisations that commissioned them.

**Why This Matters:** Red teamer names a profession whose value depends on its independence — and whose structural position compromises that independence. The existence of the role is reassuring; the conditions under which it operates are not.
