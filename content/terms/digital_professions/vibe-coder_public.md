# Vibe Coder

You describe what you want. The AI writes the code. It works — mostly. You do not know why. You do not need to. Until you do.

**Literal meaning:** Vibe coder is a term coined by AI researcher Andrej Karpathy in February 2025 to describe a mode of programming in which the developer relies almost entirely on AI-generated code, accepting outputs without understanding the underlying logic, debugging by intuition and iteration rather than by reading and reasoning about code. The "vibe" signals an aesthetic or intuitive relationship with the software rather than a technical one.

**Origin:** Karpathy introduced the term on social media as a half-ironic, half-sincere description of a practice he observed becoming common as AI coding assistants became capable of generating functional code from natural language descriptions. The term was widely adopted and debated: some welcomed it as democratisation of software creation; others flagged it as a new form of [[Deskilling]] — the loss of foundational competence that makes systems legible, debuggable, and secure.

> A person who uses AI to write code they do not fully understand — producing functional software through iteration and intuition rather than through comprehension of the underlying logic.

**The Appeal:** Vibe coding lowers the barrier to software creation genuinely and significantly. People without programming backgrounds can now build functional tools, automate tasks, and create products that previously required significant technical investment. For rapid prototyping, internal tools, and personal projects, the approach works. The democratisation is real: creative and domain expertise can now be translated into software without requiring a software engineering degree.

**The Friction:** The gap between working and understood is the problem. [[Deskilling]] — automation making foundational skills redundant — is the long-term risk: a generation of practitioners who can produce but not evaluate, who can generate but not audit. [[AI Dependency]] — structural reliance without critical reflection — is what vibe coding institutionalises at the level of professional practice. [[Cognitive Surrender]] — giving up one's own reasoning to digital systems — describes the epistemic position: the code works, the AI said so, and understanding why is optional. When vibe-coded systems fail — and they do — the person who built them lacks the knowledge to diagnose or fix them. [[Red Teamer]] is the adversarial counterpart: someone who understands systems well enough to break them intentionally; the vibe coder is someone who may break them accidentally and not know it.

**Why This Matters:** Vibe coder names a specific trade-off that AI coding tools make visible but do not resolve: access versus understanding. The profession can be everything it claims — democratising, creative, productive — and still produce infrastructure that nobody fully comprehends. That infrastructure will eventually need to be maintained, audited, or fixed.
