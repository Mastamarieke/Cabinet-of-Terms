# Ghost Worker

Behind the artificial intelligence, there are people. They label the data, review the outputs, handle the exceptions. They are not in the press release.

**Literal meaning:** Ghost workers are the people who perform the hidden human labour that makes AI and platform systems function — labelling training data, reviewing AI outputs, handling edge cases that automated systems cannot process, and performing the micro-tasks that are presented as machine intelligence. The term was coined by researchers Mary L. Gray and Siddharth Suri in their 2019 book *Ghost Work: How to Stop Silicon Valley from Building a New Global Underclass*.

**Origin:** Gray and Suri's research documented the scale of human labour concealed behind AI systems — from Amazon Mechanical Turk workers labelling images for computer vision models to the armies of contractors reviewing content moderation decisions. The work is characterised by piece-rate payment, algorithmic management, no employment benefits, and deliberate invisibility: companies have strong incentives to present their systems as automated rather than dependent on human labour.

> The invisible, piece-rate human labour that underlies AI and platform systems — performed by workers who are structurally concealed so that the systems they maintain appear autonomous.

**The Appeal:** Ghost work provides flexible income to people who might otherwise lack access to formal employment — including people in low-income countries, people with disabilities, parents with care responsibilities, and others whose lives do not fit standard employment structures. The flexibility is genuine and valued by some workers. At scale, ghost work has enabled AI systems that could not otherwise exist.

**The Friction:** The structural invisibility is the core problem. [[AI Dependency]] — structural reliance on AI systems without critical reflection — is partly sustained by the myth of machine autonomy that ghost work supports. If users and clients knew that AI outputs depend on large pools of low-paid human reviewers, the calculus of automation would change. [[Externalized Costs]] — burdens transferred from companies to workers and communities — describes the economics: ghost work externalises the labour cost of AI onto a distributed, invisible workforce without employment protections. [[Content Moderator]] is the specific ghost worker role most publicly documented; the category extends far beyond moderation into data labelling, quality assurance, and exception handling across every major AI system.

**Why This Matters:** Ghost worker dissolves the autonomy claim at the centre of AI discourse. Every time a company says "our AI does X," the ghost worker is the invisible asterisk. The profession exists because automation is never complete — and the humans filling the gaps are paid not to be seen.
