# Memecoin

The coin was a joke. People lost real money. The person who created it did not.

**Literal meaning**
*Meme* — a unit of cultural transmission, especially a viral internet image or idea. *Coin* — a cryptocurrency token. A Memecoin is a cryptocurrency built around cultural or social phenomena rather than underlying technological utility.

**Origin**
Dogecoin (2013) was the first significant memecoin — a joke cryptocurrency based on the Doge meme that gained serious valuation when Elon Musk endorsed it. The pattern has been repeated with hundreds of memecoins, including Trump-branded coins launched in 2025.

> A Memecoin is cryptocurrency whose value is derived from cultural attention and celebrity endorsement rather than technological utility — making it a vehicle for speculative transfer of wealth from buyers to early holders.

**The Appeal**
Memecoins offer participation in a cultural moment, community belonging, and the possibility of significant returns. The barrier to entry is low.

**The Friction**
[[terms/society_power/financial_fabulism_public|Financial Fabulism]] is the financial mechanism: the coin's value is a narrative, and the narrative generates real money flows. The structure is zero-sum: early holders profit from late buyers' losses. Celebrity endorsement has been documented as a systematic extraction mechanism.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Memecoins as a financial weapon using subcultural aesthetics, you see that the playful format lowers the critical guard of participants who would recognise the same structure in conventional financial instruments.
