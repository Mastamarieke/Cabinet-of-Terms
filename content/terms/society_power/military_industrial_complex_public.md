# Military-Industrial Complex

Eisenhower warned about it in his farewell address. The industry he described has since absorbed the internet.

**Literal meaning**
*Military* — armed forces. *Industrial* — large-scale production. *Complex* — an interconnected system. The entanglement of armed forces, defence industry, and government creating structural incentives for military spending.

**Origin**
Dwight D. Eisenhower coined the term in his January 1961 farewell address, warning against "unwarranted influence, whether sought or unsought, by the military-industrial complex." It has since become foundational in political economy.

> The Military-Industrial Complex is the structural entanglement of military institutions, defence industry, and government that creates self-perpetuating incentives for military expansion and conflict.

**The Appeal**
Defence industry employment, technological development, and national security provision are genuine goods the complex delivers.

**The Friction**
The structural incentive is independent of any individual actor's intentions: the complex profits from conflict regardless of whether conflict is necessary. [[terms/society_power/revolving_door_public|Revolving Door]] is the personnel mechanism. [[terms/society_power/digital_military_industrial_complex_public|Digital-Military-Industrial Complex]] extends the concept to Big Tech.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see the Military-Industrial Complex as a structural incentive system, you see that the question is not who wants war — it is which institutions profit from the preparation for it.
