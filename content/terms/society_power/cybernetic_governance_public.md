# Cybernetic Governance

The decision was made by the system. No one is responsible. No one can be appealed to.

**Literal meaning**
*Cybernetic* — relating to regulatory systems, from Greek kubernetes (steersman). *Governance* — systems by which power is exercised. Cybernetic Governance describes the replacement of human decision-making with automated algorithmic systems.

**Origin**
The concept builds on Norbert Wiener's cybernetics (1948). Contemporary cybernetic governance describes welfare algorithms, border control systems, predictive policing, and automated content moderation — government functions outsourced to automated decision-making.

> Cybernetic Governance is the replacement of human judgment in governance with automated algorithmic systems — removing accountability while maintaining power.

**The Appeal**
Automated governance promises consistency, efficiency, and freedom from human bias. Processing millions of welfare claims by algorithm is faster and cheaper than human review.

**The Friction**
[[terms/privacy_compliance/algorithmic_violence_public|Algorithmic Violence]] is the consequence: automated systems produce harm without individual accountability. The appeal of efficiency conceals the removal of contestability: you cannot argue with an algorithm.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Cybernetic Governance as the replacement of accountability rather than improvement of efficiency, you see that the question is not whether the system is accurate — it is who bears the cost when it is wrong.
