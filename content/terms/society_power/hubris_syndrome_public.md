# Hubris Syndrome

The diagnosis is not about arrogance. It is about what sustained, unchallenged power does to judgment.

**Literal meaning**
*Hubris* — from Greek: excessive pride. *Syndrome* — a recognised cluster of symptoms. Hubris Syndrome is David Owen's concept describing the characteristic deterioration of judgment in people with sustained, unchecked power.

**Origin**
David Owen — British politician and physician — developed Hubris Syndrome in a 2007 paper and book, drawing on case studies of political leaders. He described a cluster of traits: contempt for others' advice, loss of contact with reality, and reckless actions.

> Hubris Syndrome is the documented pattern of judgment deterioration in people with sustained, unchecked power — a psychological-political concept with direct applications to tech leadership.

**The Appeal**
The concept has explanatory power: it describes why capable people with genuine achievements become catastrophically poor decision-makers when accountability structures are removed.

**The Friction**
[[terms/society_power/founder_mode_public|Founder Mode]] — founder authority above governance — removes the accountability structures that Hubris Syndrome research identifies as preventative. [[terms/society_power/muskism_public|Muskism]] exemplifies the pattern.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Hubris Syndrome as a structural outcome of unchecked power, you see that the solution is not finding better people — it is maintaining accountability structures that no individual should be able to remove.
