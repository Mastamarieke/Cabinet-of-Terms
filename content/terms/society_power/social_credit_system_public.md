# Social Credit System

The Chinese version is described as dystopia. The Western version is described as fintech. The scoring logic is structurally similar.

**Literal meaning**
*Social* — relating to society. *Credit* — a score of trustworthiness. *System* — an organised structure. A Social Credit System scores citizens on behavioural criteria and attaches rewards or penalties.

**Origin**
China's Social Credit System was announced in 2014. Western coverage has often conflated multiple distinct regional programmes. Western equivalents include credit scoring, insurance premium algorithms, and employment screening tools using behavioural data to make consequential decisions.

> A Social Credit System is the infrastructure for scoring citizen behaviour and attaching material consequences — a form of cybernetic governance that exists in different configurations across political systems.

**The Appeal**
Scoring systems can increase accountability and reduce information asymmetries that enable fraud.

**The Friction**
[[terms/platform_mechanisms/biometric_surveillance_public|Biometric Surveillance]] provides the data infrastructure. The Western framing of Chinese social credit as uniquely dystopian obscures how similar scoring logic operates in Western financial and employment systems with equivalent consequences and less accountability.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Social Credit Systems as a governance logic rather than a Chinese peculiarity, you see that behavioural scoring with material consequences is a global infrastructure — and that its legitimacy depends on who controls it and who is accountable for its errors.
