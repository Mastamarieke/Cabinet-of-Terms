# Reconstruction Economy

The contractor builds what the bomb destroys. This is not a paradox. It is a business model.

**Literal meaning**
*Reconstruction* — rebuilding something destroyed. *Economy* — a system of production and exchange. The market that exists because destruction has created the need for rebuilding.

**Origin**
The concept describes the documented relationship between military destruction and private reconstruction contracts — from post-WWII Germany to post-invasion Iraq. Naomi Klein documented the "shock doctrine" version: disasters and conflicts as opportunities for private capital.

> The Reconstruction Economy is the market created by destruction — where the same interests that profit from conflict also profit from rebuilding, creating structural incentives for the former.

**The Appeal**
Reconstruction is necessary after destruction. Private sector capacity to rebuild is real and valuable.

**The Friction**
[[public/society_power/military_industrial_complex_public|Military-Industrial Complex]] creates the destruction; the Reconstruction Economy profits from the aftermath. The same contractors often participate in both. [[public/society_power/revolving_door_public|Revolving Door]] personnel move between defence, government, and reconstruction contracts.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see the Reconstruction Economy as a structural incentive, you see that the profitability of rebuilding affects decisions about destroying.
