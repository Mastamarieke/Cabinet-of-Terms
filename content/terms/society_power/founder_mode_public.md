# Founder Mode

The founder knows best. The managers should get out of the way. The governance structures are friction.

**Literal meaning**
*Founder* — the person who created and initially led a company. *Mode* — an operational state. Founder Mode is Paul Graham's 2024 concept advocating for founders to maintain direct, interventionist control rather than delegating to professional managers.

**Origin**
Paul Graham (Y Combinator) published an essay in 2024 titled "Founder Mode," describing how founders like Brian Chesky succeeded by maintaining deep involvement. The essay was widely cited as validating authoritarian leadership styles in tech.

> Founder Mode is the ideology that founder authority should supersede conventional governance and management structures — positioning accountability mechanisms as obstacles to success.

**The Appeal**
Founders with genuine vision can be more effective decision-makers than professional managers without skin in the game. Direct involvement avoids bureaucratic distance from reality.

**The Friction**
[[terms/society_power/hubris_syndrome_public|Hubris Syndrome]] describes what sustained, unchecked power does to judgment. [[terms/society_power/muskism_public|Muskism]] is Founder Mode applied to political infrastructure.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Founder Mode as an ideology that removes accountability, you see that the choice between governance and founder authority is also a choice about what happens when the founder is wrong.
