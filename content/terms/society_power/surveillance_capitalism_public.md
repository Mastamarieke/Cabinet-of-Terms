# Surveillance Capitalism

Your clicks, your pauses, your hesitations — all of it is data. All of it is someone else's raw material.

**Literal meaning**
*Surveillance* — systematic observation and data collection. *Capitalism* — economic system based on private ownership and profit. Surveillance capitalism is capitalism that extracts value from the observation of human behaviour.

**Origin**
Shoshana Zuboff coined and developed the term in her 2019 book *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*. She traces its origins to Google's discovery that user behavioural data — initially a byproduct of search — was more valuable than the search service itself.

> Surveillance capitalism is the economic logic by which human behaviour is captured as data, analysed for patterns, and sold as predictions — without users' meaningful knowledge or consent.

**The Appeal**
The services built on surveillance capitalism are often genuinely useful: search, maps, social connection, personalised content. The data extraction is the price, but it is largely invisible.

**The Friction**
The raw material is not your data — it is your future behaviour. [[terms/platform_mechanisms/attention_economy_public|Attention Economy]] logic means the system needs to predict and influence what you will do next. Behavioural modification is not a side effect; it is the product.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see surveillance capitalism as an economic logic rather than a privacy problem, you see that regulation focused on data protection misses the point. The issue is not that your data is collected — it is what the collection is for.
