# Broligarchy

The consolidation of power at the top of the tech industry has a social structure. It looks like a friend group.

**Literal meaning**
*Bro* — informal term for male friend, with connotations of fraternity and exclusivity. *Oligarchy* — rule by a small group. Broligarchy describes the tech oligarchy as a male social network.

**Origin**
The term gained currency from 2024, describing the visible consolidation of power among a small group of predominantly white male tech billionaires — Musk, Bezos, Zuckerberg, Altman, and others — whose coordination and shared political positions constituted a recognisable social formation.

> Broligarchy is the tech oligarchy understood as a male social network — power concentrated through informal homosocial bonds that exclude by gender, race, and class simultaneously.

**The Appeal**
Naming the social structure of power makes it more legible and more contestable.

**The Friction**
[[public/society_power/muskism_public|Muskism]] is the political programme; Broligarchy is the social structure. [[public/society_power/founder_mode_public|Founder Mode]] is the organisational ideology. The "bro" element is not incidental: it describes exclusion mechanisms — informal networks that produce formal consequences.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Broligarchy as a social structure rather than an accidental demographic pattern, you see that the concentration of power at the top of the tech industry reproduces itself through exclusion.
