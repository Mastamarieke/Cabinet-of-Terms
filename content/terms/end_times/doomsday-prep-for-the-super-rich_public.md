# Doomsday Prep for the Super-Rich

Bunkers in New Zealand. Private airstrips. Staff kept on retainer. The collapse is not a political failure to be prevented — it is a logistical problem to be survived.

**Literal meaning:** Doomsday prep for the super-rich describes the practice of wealthy individuals — primarily Silicon Valley investors, tech founders, and financiers — investing in private survival infrastructure: bunkers, remote properties, private security forces, food stockpiles, and exit routes. It is distinguished from ordinary prepping by its scale, sophistication, and the class of people engaged in it.

**Origin:** The phenomenon was documented in detail by journalist Evan Osnos in a 2017 *New Yorker* article, which revealed that significant numbers of Silicon Valley billionaires were purchasing properties in New Zealand, building underground bunkers, and developing private contingency plans for societal collapse. The practice intensified around 2020 and continued into the mid-2020s as climate change, political polarisation, and AI risk anxiety converged.

> The practice of wealthy individuals building private survival infrastructure against civilisational collapse — treating catastrophe as an individual logistics problem rather than a collective political one.

**The Appeal:** Risk management is rational, and wealthy people have more capacity to manage risk. For individuals who genuinely believe civilisational risks are non-trivial — AI misalignment, pandemic, political collapse — preparing makes internal sense. Some of the same people investing in bunkers are also funding AI safety research or climate mitigation. The two impulses are not mutually exclusive.

**The Friction:** The political logic of the practice contradicts its stated concern. If collapse is coming because collective systems are failing, building private exit routes accelerates that failure — removing the people with the most resources and influence from the shared systems that need them. [[Armageddon Complex]] — apocalyptic thinking that justifies power accumulation — describes the self-reinforcing loop: believing in collapse justifies exit; exit makes collapse more likely. [[Fortress Futurism]] — technology as fortress — is the design philosophy. [[Prepperism]] — survival as identity project, available to a much wider population at much lower cost — is the mass-market version of the same anxiety, without the New Zealand property portfolio. The gap between the two reveals what the practice is actually about: not survival, but survival *with existing privileges intact*.

**Why This Matters:** Doomsday prep for the super-rich makes legible a political position usually left unsaid: that civilisational risk is someone else's problem to solve, and the wealthy have already made other arrangements. That arrangement is itself a political act.
