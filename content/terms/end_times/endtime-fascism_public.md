# Endtime Fascism

When the end is near enough, anything done to prevent it becomes justified. That is not an argument — it is a mechanism.

**Literal meaning:** Endtime fascism describes the fusion of fascist political structures with eschatological — end-times — ideology. It names the political form that emerges when an authoritarian movement is legitimised not by promises of order or prosperity but by the claim that civilisation is ending and extraordinary measures are therefore necessary. The term foregrounds the role of apocalyptic urgency as a justificatory engine for the suspension of rights, procedures, and limits.

**Origin:** The term draws on scholarship examining the relationship between millenarianism and political violence — including Roger Griffin's work on fascism as a form of "palingenetic ultranationalism," a movement organised around the death and rebirth of a nation or civilisation. Its contemporary application is associated with researchers examining how digital platforms accelerate the radicalisation of eschatological political movements and how AI-enabled information environments enable new forms of authoritarian consolidation.

> The political form in which authoritarian power is justified by apocalyptic urgency — where the end of civilisation is the premise that suspends all limits on what may be done to prevent or survive it.

**The Appeal:** If civilisation is genuinely ending — through demographic collapse, cultural replacement, technological catastrophe, or enemy action — then the case for extraordinary measures has internal logic. The appeal is not to cruelty but to necessity: we would not choose these methods if the situation were not so dire. The urgency is experienced as real, the threat as existential, and the response as proportionate.

**The Friction:** The eschatological frame is structurally self-sealing. Evidence that the threat is less severe than claimed becomes evidence of how deeply the enemy has penetrated. Procedural limits that prevent authoritarian consolidation become obstacles placed by those who want civilisation to fail. [[Millenarianism]] — end-times thinking as political-religious structure — provides the unfalsifiable core. [[Disaster Nationalism]] provides the crisis frame that makes populations receptive. [[Armageddon Complex]] names the self-serving dimension: power accumulation justified by the very emergency it benefits from. [[Dark MAGA]] is one contemporary aesthetic form this takes; [[Pro-natalism]] in its most ideological form is one of its policy expressions.

**Why This Matters:** Endtime fascism makes visible the mechanism by which emergency powers become permanent — not through explicit authoritarian intent alone, but through a framework in which the emergency is by definition never resolved enough to restore limits. Once you recognise the structure, the permanence of the emergency becomes legible as a feature, not a failure.
