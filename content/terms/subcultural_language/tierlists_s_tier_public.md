# Tierlists / S-tier

Everything can be ranked. Films, foods, friends. S-tier means the best. F-tier means forget it.

**Literal meaning**
*Tier* — a level in a ranked hierarchy. *S-tier* — the top tier, above A through F. Tierlists are ranked hierarchies applied to any category of thing — originally games and gaming characters, now applied to almost anything.

**Origin**
Tierlists originated in competitive gaming communities in the 1990s–2000s, ranking character choices by competitive viability. The format spread to YouTube and TikTok as a content format: creators rank films, albums, foods, celebrities, and concepts. The vocabulary migrated from gaming to general cultural discourse.

> Tierlists are the gamification of cultural and social evaluation — applying ranked hierarchy logic to everything from media to people.

**The Appeal**
Ranking is inherently engaging and shareable. Tierlists generate disagreement — and disagreement generates comments. The format is simple and endlessly applicable.

**The Friction**
[[public/platform_mechanisms/gamification_creep_public|Gamification Creep]] — game logic entering non-game contexts — is the mechanism. Applied to people, tierlist logic reproduces [[public/gender_identity/smv_public|SMV]] — Sexual Market Value — thinking: human worth as a ranked score. The vocabulary normalises hierarchical evaluation as a natural mode of relating to the world.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see tierlists as a gamification format rather than a neutral ranking exercise, you see how the habit of scoring and ranking — originally a competitive game mechanic — shapes how worth and value are thought about across domains.
