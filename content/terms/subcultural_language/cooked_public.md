# Cooked

One word. Absolute certainty. No appeal. Cooked.

**Literal meaning**
*Cooked* — slang for irreversibly ruined, finished, beyond recovery. Applied to people, situations, arguments, or reputations that have failed so completely that no recovery is possible.

**Origin**
Cooked in this sense derives from cooking metaphors for completion — something fully cooked is done. The term entered Gen Z internet vocabulary around 2020–2022 and spread rapidly as a verdictive term: one-word judgment, no elaboration needed.

> Cooked is the subcultural term for irreversible failure — a definitive platform verdict delivered in a single word, closing off further discussion.

**The Appeal**
Compressed, absolute language is efficient in fast-moving social media discourse. One word conveys a shared cultural judgment that would take several sentences to explain.

**The Friction**
The verdict function of "cooked" — its finality — is part of what makes it function as a platform enforcement mechanism. It does not describe; it closes. Applied to people rather than situations, it operates as social exclusion language. The one-word format resists nuance by design.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see "cooked" as a verdictive speech act rather than a descriptive term, you see how compressed subcultural language shapes social judgment — and how the efficiency of one-word verdicts accelerates rather than enables genuine evaluation.
