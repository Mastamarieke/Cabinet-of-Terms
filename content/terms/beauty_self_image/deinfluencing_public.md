# Deinfluencing

You do not need this product. The influencer telling you this has 2 million followers.

**Literal meaning**
*De-* — reversal prefix. *Influencing* — the practice of shaping behaviour through platform reach. Deinfluencing is the TikTok trend of creators telling audiences not to buy products — the critique of overconsumption distributed through the consumption infrastructure.

**Origin**
Deinfluencing emerged as a TikTok trend in early 2023. Brands quickly adopted the format and influencers used it to build credibility while maintaining commercial relationships.

> Deinfluencing is the platform-distributed critique of overconsumption — a counter-movement that reproduces the engagement logic it claims to resist.

**The Appeal**
Honest product criticism is genuinely valuable. Deinfluencing can save audiences money and reduce unnecessary consumption.

**The Friction**
[[terms/appropriated_counter_force/digital_detox_public|Digital Detox]] logic applies: the counter-movement is absorbed into the system it critiques. Deinfluencing content is optimised for engagement, distributed through algorithms, and monetisable. The critique of influencer culture becomes a new influencer category.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see deinfluencing as recuperation rather than resistance, you see how the platform economy absorbs its own critique — and how authenticity itself becomes a marketable quality.
