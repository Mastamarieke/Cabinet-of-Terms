# FOBO

You chose the restaurant. Then you checked the reviews for the one next door. You are still deciding.

**Literal meaning**
*FOBO* — Fear of Better Options. An acronym for the anxiety produced by too many choices, particularly in contexts where alternatives are continuously surfaced.

**Origin**
Patrick McGinnis coined FOBO as a companion to FOMO in a 2014 Harvard Business Review article. It has become particularly associated with dating app culture and consumer choice overload.

> FOBO is the decision paralysis produced by an abundance of continuously surfaced options — an anxiety enabled by platform architectures that always show the next alternative.

**The Appeal**
More options should mean better outcomes. The surfacing of alternatives is presented as a service.

**The Friction**
[[public/platform_mechanisms/recommender_systems_public|Recommender Systems]] continuously surface alternatives. Dating apps, streaming services, and e-commerce platforms are designed to show more options. The architecture that produces FOBO also profits from the continued browsing it generates.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see FOBO as a designed condition rather than personal indecisiveness, you see that the platform benefits from your hesitation.
