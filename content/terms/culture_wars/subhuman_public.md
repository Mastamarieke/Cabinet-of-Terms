# Subhuman

The word has a history. Every time it has been used, it has preceded something worse.

**Literal meaning**
*Sub-* — below. *Human* — of the human species. Subhuman explicitly places a person or group below the threshold of humanity.

**Origin**
The term has documented use in genocide discourse — Nazi propaganda, Rwandan radio broadcasts preceding the 1994 genocide. Its contemporary circulation in digital spaces has been documented by hate speech researchers.

> Subhuman is explicit dehumanisation language — a term with documented historical use as a precursor to organised violence, now circulating in digital political discourse.

**The Appeal**
There is no legitimate appeal — the term has no benign use case.

**The Friction**
[[terms/culture_wars/dehumanization_public|Dehumanization]] describes the psychological process. Subhuman is the linguistic expression. Research on hate speech documents that dehumanising language precedes and enables violence.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Subhuman as a historical term with documented consequences, you see that its presence in digital discourse is not a new phenomenon but a continuation of a documented pattern.
