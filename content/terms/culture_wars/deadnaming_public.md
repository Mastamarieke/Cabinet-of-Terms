# Deadnaming

The name you no longer use is still indexed. The search engine does not forget.

**Literal meaning**
*Dead name* — the birth name used before a transgender person's transition. *Deadnaming* — using that name after the person has adopted a new name.

**Origin**
Deadnaming entered LGBTQ+ and mainstream discourse in the 2010s. The term describes both interpersonal behaviour and a structural problem: digital permanence means old names persist in archives even after legal name changes.

> Deadnaming is the use of a transgender person's pre-transition name — a behaviour that is both interpersonal and infrastructural, as digital systems preserve old names indefinitely.

**The Appeal**
This term has no "appeal" in the standard sense. Its analytical value is in revealing how digital permanence intersects with identity.

**The Friction**
[[public/privacy_compliance/privacy_washing_public|Privacy Washing]] is relevant: the right to be forgotten is legally established in GDPR but difficult to enforce against search indexes and archives. Deadnaming is partly a technical problem: systems that treat names as permanent identifiers are not designed with identity change in mind.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see deadnaming as a structural infrastructure problem, you see that the design of identity systems has direct consequences for lived safety.
