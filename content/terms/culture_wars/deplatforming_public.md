# Deplatforming

The account is gone. The content is gone. The audience is not.

**Literal meaning**
*De-* — prefix indicating removal. *Platform* — the hosting infrastructure. Deplatforming is the removal of a user, account, or content from a platform by its operators.

**Origin**
The practice predates the term — platforms have always moderated content. The word became politically charged after high-profile removals of far-right figures from Twitter, YouTube, and Facebook between 2017 and 2021.

> Deplatforming is the removal of users or content from digital platforms — a moderation tool whose effectiveness and political implications remain genuinely contested.

**The Appeal**
Removing harmful actors from large platforms reduces their immediate reach and can disrupt coordination. Research on the deplatforming of specific communities suggests measurable reductions in activity and influence.

**The Friction**
Deplatforming does not eliminate the underlying conditions that produced the actor. Removed communities typically migrate to alternative platforms, sometimes with increased radicalisation. The measure addresses visibility, not cause.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see deplatforming as a symptom response rather than a structural intervention, you see that the debate about whether it "works" is the wrong debate. The question is what problem it is actually solving — and for whom.
