# Social Contagion

The scientific term describes how behaviours spread through populations. The political deployment describes how trans identity spreads through friend groups. These are different claims.

**Literal meaning**
*Social* — spread through social networks. *Contagion* — transmission of a condition from person to person. In political use: the claim that transgender identity spreads through peer influence.

**Origin**
Social contagion has legitimate scientific uses. Its application to transgender identity emerged from a contested 2018 study and spread in conservative media as a framework for opposing gender-affirming care.

> Social Contagion, in its political deployment, is the medical framing of transgender identity as a contagious phenomenon — using scientific vocabulary to pathologise identity without scientific support.

**The Appeal**
Parental concern about peer influence on adolescent identity is real. The scientific framing lends apparent authority.

**The Friction**
The scientific evidence for "social contagion" of transgender identity is contested. [[public/culture_wars/woke_mind_virus_public|Woke Mind Virus]] uses the same rhetorical structure at a broader scale.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Social Contagion as borrowed scientific vocabulary, you see how medical framing can lend authority to political claims regardless of the evidence.
