# Seasteading

If you cannot change the rules, build a platform in international waters where the rules do not apply.

**Literal meaning:** Seasteading is the concept of creating permanent, self-governing communities on platforms in international waters — outside the jurisdiction of any existing state. The term combines *sea* with *homesteading*, the American frontier practice of claiming unoccupied land. The project frames the ocean as a new frontier for political experimentation: a space where new governance systems can be built from scratch, free from inherited regulations.

**Origin:** The Seasteading Institute was founded in 2008 by Patri Friedman — grandson of economist Milton Friedman — and funded by Peter Thiel, who contributed $1.7 million in initial funding. Thiel has written explicitly that he no longer believes democracy and freedom are compatible, and that seasteading offers an escape from that incompatibility. Despite significant media attention and investment, no seastead has been successfully established; a partnership with French Polynesia dissolved in 2018.

> The project of building permanent communities in international waters to escape state jurisdiction — framed as political innovation, functioning as elite exit from democratic accountability.

**The Appeal:** Seasteading takes seriously a genuine frustration: that existing political systems are often rigid, slow, and resistant to reform. The idea that new governance models could be tested and iterated — like software — in controlled environments has real intellectual appeal. For political theorists, the concept raises interesting questions about the relationship between geography and sovereignty.

**The Friction:** The gap between the rhetoric of experimentation and the sociology of who proposes it is the friction. Seasteading is not proposed by communities seeking escape from authoritarianism — it is proposed by some of the wealthiest individuals in liberal democracies. [[Fortress Futurism]] — technology as fortress, with origins in apartheid South Africa — describes the underlying logic: building protected enclaves rather than reforming shared systems. [[Sovereignty as a Service]] — state sovereignty outsourced to private tech infrastructure — names the governance model that replaces democratic accountability. The frontier metaphor is not accidental: frontiers in American history were never empty spaces but occupied territories whose existing inhabitants did not set the terms.

**Why This Matters:** Seasteading externalises a political position that is usually left implicit: that the wealthy should have the option of exit from collective decisions they dislike. Making that position concrete makes it contestable.
