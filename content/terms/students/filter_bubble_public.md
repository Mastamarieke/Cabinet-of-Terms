# Filter Bubble

You and your colleague both searched the same term. You got different results. Neither of you knew.

**Literal meaning**
*Filter* — a mechanism that selects what passes through. *Bubble* — an enclosed space insulated from outside. Filter Bubble is Eli Pariser's term for the personalised information environment created by algorithmic curation.

**Origin**
Eli Pariser coined the term in his 2011 book *The Filter Bubble*, documenting how personalisation algorithms on Google, Facebook, and other platforms create individually tailored information environments that reinforce existing beliefs and preferences.

> A Filter Bubble is the personalised information environment produced by recommendation algorithms — showing each user a version of the world shaped by their prior behaviour.

**The Appeal**
Personalisation is genuinely useful: relevant content is more valuable than irrelevant content. The algorithm learns what you engage with and serves more of it.

**The Friction**
[[terms/platform_mechanisms/recommender_systems_public|Recommender Systems]] — optimising for engagement — learn that content confirming existing beliefs generates more engagement than content challenging them. The bubble is not visible from inside: you see a curated version of the world that appears complete. [[terms/students/echo_chamber_public|Echo Chamber]] is the social version of the same phenomenon.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see the filter bubble as an infrastructure condition rather than a personal choice, you see that exiting it requires deliberate effort against the grain of every design incentive.
