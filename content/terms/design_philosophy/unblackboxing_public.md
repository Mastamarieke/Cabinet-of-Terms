# Unblackboxing

You use it every day. You have no idea how it works. Someone decided that was fine.

**Literal meaning**
*Un-* — reversal. *Blackboxing* — the process by which the internal workings of a system become opaque, taken for granted, and invisible to users. Unblackboxing is making those workings visible again.

**Origin**
The concept draws on science and technology studies — particularly Bruno Latour's work on how technologies become "black boxes" as they stabilise and are accepted without question. Applied to digital systems, unblackboxing describes the analytical practice of opening those boxes.

> Unblackboxing is the practice of making hidden mechanisms visible — asking how a system works, who built it, what it optimises for, and whose interests it serves.

**The Appeal**
Most systems work well enough that understanding their internals is unnecessary. Blackboxing is a feature, not just a bug: it reduces cognitive load and enables use at scale.

**The Friction**
When the hidden mechanism causes harm — when the algorithm amplifies outrage, when the AI hallucinates confidently, when the platform design exploits psychology — opacity protects the system, not the user. [[terms/society_power/surveillance_capitalism_public|Surveillance Capitalism]] depends on users not asking how the extraction works.

**Why This Matters**
Unblackboxing is the analytical foundation of this vault. Every term here is an attempt to open a box — to ask what a word does rather than just what it means, and whose interests it serves when you accept it without question.
