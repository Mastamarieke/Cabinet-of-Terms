# Training Run

A model like the one generating this text was trained once — in a process that consumed more energy than most households use in years.

**Literal meaning:** A training run is the single, computationally intensive process during which an AI model learns from vast datasets. It happens before the model is ever used — but it accounts for a substantial share of the model's total environmental cost.

**Origin:** The term comes from machine learning engineering practice and has been in use since the early days of neural network research. It gained broader analytical significance when researchers began publishing energy and emissions figures for specific training runs — most notably GPT-3 (2020) and subsequent large language models — making the scale of pre-deployment energy use publicly legible for the first time.

> The one-time but enormous process of building an AI model from data — concentrated, high-energy, and completed before any user interaction begins.

**The Appeal:** For AI developers, the training run is where capability is created. The investment is finite and purposeful: spend energy once, deploy indefinitely. Amortised across millions of users and billions of queries, the per-interaction cost can be made to look small. This logic is not dishonest — it reflects how the economics genuinely work.

**The Friction:** The amortisation logic conceals the absolute scale. A single training run for a frontier model can emit hundreds of tonnes of CO₂ — a figure that does not shrink because many people benefit. And training is not a one-time event: models are retrained, fine-tuned, and replaced in rapid cycles. [[Externalized Costs]] — environmental burdens placed on communities rather than absorbed by companies — mean that the communities near the data centres bear the concentrated impact while the benefits are distributed elsewhere. The run ends; the heat, water use, and grid pressure do not.

**Why This Matters:** The training run makes visible a structural asymmetry in AI's environmental accounting: cost is concentrated in place and time, benefit is diffuse and global. Once you know where to look, "a new model was released today" becomes a different kind of sentence.
