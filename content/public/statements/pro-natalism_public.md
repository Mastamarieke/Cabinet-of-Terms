# Pro-natalism

He has ten children. He says civilisation depends on having more. The demographics are a crisis. The solution is reproduction — specifically, the right people reproducing.

**Literal meaning:** Pro-natalism is the political and ideological position that promotes higher birth rates as a social and civilisational good. In its contemporary Silicon Valley and nationalist variants, it frames declining birth rates in wealthy countries as an existential threat — to civilisation, to a culture, to a specific vision of the future — and advocates policy, social pressure, or personal example to reverse them.

**Origin:** Pro-natalist policies have existed across the political spectrum — from socialist states to fascist regimes — throughout the twentieth century. Its contemporary iteration in tech culture is associated particularly with Elon Musk, who has publicly linked his own fertility choices to civilisational concern, and with Simone and Malcolm Collins, who run an explicitly pro-natalist project framed around preserving what they describe as the values and genetics of their community. The framing has migrated from demographic policy into identity and ideology.

> The ideological position that birth rates are a civilisational emergency requiring intervention — where reproduction becomes a political act and the question of *whose* reproduction is rarely far from the surface.

**The Appeal:** Demographic change is real, and its consequences for pension systems, care infrastructure, and economic productivity are genuinely complex. For people who believe in a particular cultural or civilisational project, the anxiety about its continuation is not irrational. Some pro-natalist advocacy is simply about removing barriers — childcare costs, housing, parental leave — that make having children difficult.

**The Friction:** The contemporary tech-inflected version rarely stays at the level of barrier removal. [[Millenarianism]] — end-times thinking as political-religious structure — provides the urgency: not a policy problem but a civilisational race. The implicit question — *whose* birth rate is the concern — is where the ideology reveals itself. [[Endtime Fascism]] — fascism legitimised by eschatological urgency — has historically been inseparable from pro-natalist ideology aimed at specific populations. [[Disaster Nationalism]] provides the crisis frame that makes reproduction a political obligation rather than a personal choice. When a billionaire frames his ten children as a civilisational contribution, the question of which civilisation, and for whom, is not incidental.

**Why This Matters:** Pro-natalism makes visible the political content embedded in what appears to be demographic concern. Once you know the term, "birth rates are falling" is never a neutral statement — it is always also a statement about which births, and who should be making them.
