# SMV (Sexual Market Value)

You're told your worth can be measured. Not by your character, but by your face, your body, your status. And suddenly you're calculating your score.

**Literal meaning**
*SMV* stands for Sexual Market Value — the idea that human attractiveness can be quantified like a stock price.

**Origin**
Emerging from the manosphere in the late 2000s, the term borrowed language from economics and applied it to dating. It gained traction on forums, then spread to TikTok and Instagram where "rating" videos became content.

> SMV is the quantification of human attractiveness as market value — turning people into products and relationships into transactions.

**The Appeal**
For someone who feels invisible, SMV offers clarity. [[public/gender_identity/hypergamy_public|Hypergamy]] — the strategy of seeking partners of higher status — gives you a goal. [[public/gender_identity/looksmaxxing_public|Looksmaxxing]] — optimising appearance as an identity project — gives you a path. The confusion of dating becomes a scorecard. You know where you stand. The appeal is powerful because uncertainty is exhausting.

**The Friction**
But the scorecard that promises clarity also traps you. Your value is no longer yours — it's assigned by others. [[public/platform_mechanisms/recommender_systems_public|Recommender Systems]] — algorithms that feed you more of what keeps you watching — turn the ecosystem into a pipeline. A man searching for confidence gets routed toward [[public/gender_identity/manosphere_public|Manosphere]] content, where rejection is framed as systemic injustice. A woman searching for purpose gets routed toward [[public/gender_identity/womanosphere_public|Womanosphere]] content, where independence is framed as failure. The path feels like self-improvement. It is design.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see SMV as a market logic rather than a personal truth, you see how the same platforms that sell dating apps also sell the anxiety they create. The score is not real — it's a product.
