# Fact Checker

The profession exists because the information system is producing more false claims than people can assess on their own. That is not a solution — that is a symptom treated as a job.

**Literal meaning:** A fact checker is a professional who verifies the accuracy of claims made in journalism, political discourse, social media, and public communication. Fact checking as a journalistic practice has existed for decades within newsrooms. As a distinct, publicly visible profession — operating through dedicated organisations that publish verdicts on specific claims — it emerged primarily in the 2010s in response to the scale of misinformation in digital environments.

**Origin:** Dedicated fact-checking organisations — PolitiFact (2007), FactCheck.org (2003), Snopes (1994, expanded in the 2000s), Full Fact (2010) — emerged as the volume of verifiable false claims in public discourse outpaced what traditional journalism could address. The profession gained further urgency with the 2016 US election, the COVID-19 pandemic, and the proliferation of AI-generated content from 2022 onward. In each case, the fact checker's caseload expanded faster than the profession's capacity.

> A professional who verifies the accuracy of public claims — a role that grows in proportion to the volume of misinformation the information ecosystem produces and distributes.

**The Appeal:** Fact checking provides something genuinely valuable: documented, sourced verdicts on specific claims, produced by people with time and expertise to investigate what most audiences cannot. The best fact-checking organisations have developed rigorous methodologies, transparent corrections policies, and genuine accountability. Their work matters for courts, regulators, and journalists building on contested records.

**The Friction:** The profession is structurally reactive and structurally outpaced. [[Flood the Zone]] — information overload as strategic destabilisation — produces false claims faster than fact checkers can address them. [[AI Hallucination]] — AI systems producing convincing falsehoods — scales the production of plausible misinformation beyond human capacity to monitor. [[Bot Farms]] — networks of automated accounts for information manipulation — distribute claims at speeds that make pre-publication verification impossible. Fact checking responds to the symptom; the systems producing the symptom are not the fact checker's to reform. There is also a structural irony: fact checking is most consumed by people who already distrust the claims being checked, and least consumed by those most likely to believe them.

**Why This Matters:** The fact checker is a profession that measures the failure of an information ecosystem. More fact checkers are not a sign of a healthier information environment — they are a sign of a more broken one. Once you know that, the growth of the profession becomes a diagnostic rather than a reassurance.
