# Prompt Engineer

The AI can do it — if you know exactly how to ask. Knowing how to ask has become a profession.

**Literal meaning:** A prompt engineer is a person who designs, refines, and optimises the inputs — prompts — given to AI language models to produce desired outputs. The role involves understanding how models respond to different phrasings, structures, constraints, and examples; developing reusable prompt templates; and systematically testing variations. It emerged as a recognised profession between 2022 and 2024, with salaries at major tech companies reaching six figures.

**Origin:** The term entered common usage with the public release of large language models from 2022 onward. As organisations began integrating AI into workflows, a gap became apparent between what models could do and what users could reliably elicit — a gap that skilled prompt design could partially close. The profession was enthusiastically promoted by AI companies, job boards, and media as a new career path. By 2024, it was also being questioned: as models improved, some observers argued that the profession would become obsolete, absorbed into the models themselves.

> A person who designs inputs to AI systems to reliably produce useful outputs — a role that mediates the gap between machine capability and human communication.

**The Appeal:** Prompt engineering is a real skill that produces real value. Organisations that invest in it get more reliable, more accurate, and more useful AI outputs. The profession also creates something genuinely new: a set of practices and vocabulary for systematic human-AI collaboration that did not exist before. For individuals entering a labour market disrupted by AI, it offers a route that works with the technology rather than against it.

**The Friction:** The profession sits on unstable ground. [[Deskilling]] — automation making skills redundant — is the risk that prompt engineering simultaneously addresses and embodies: it is a skill built on top of a technology that is actively being improved to require less of it. [[AI Dependency]] — structural reliance without critical reflection — is what prompt engineering can reproduce at scale: organisations that optimise for AI outputs without maintaining the underlying expertise to evaluate them. [[Vibe Coder]] — Andrej Karpathy's 2025 term for programming without understanding — describes the adjacent risk: fluency in prompting without understanding what the model is doing or why. [[Ghost Worker]] is the structural comparison: like ghost work, prompt engineering is human labour that makes AI appear more capable than it is, and may be similarly rendered invisible as models improve.

**Why This Matters:** Prompt engineer names the moment when talking to machines became a skilled profession — and raises the question of what that skill is actually for. Is it a bridge to better human-AI collaboration, or a workaround for systems that are not yet good enough to not need it?
