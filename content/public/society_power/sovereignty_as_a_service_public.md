# Sovereignty as a Service

The state runs on AWS. The military uses Microsoft. The passport system is Palantir. Sovereignty has been outsourced.

**Literal meaning**
*Sovereignty* — supreme authority within a territory. *As a Service* — the SaaS model: functionality delivered through a third-party platform. Sovereignty as a Service describes state functions delivered through private infrastructure.

**Origin**
A documented trend: governments outsourcing critical infrastructure — tax systems, border control, welfare payments, military logistics — to private technology companies including Amazon, Microsoft, Palantir, and Google.

> Sovereignty as a Service is the outsourcing of state functions to private technology infrastructure — a condition in which political authority depends on private commercial relationships.

**The Appeal**
Private sector technology is often more capable, faster, and cheaper than in-house government development. The efficiency gains are real.

**The Friction**
[[public/privacy_compliance/vendor_lock_in_public|Vendor Lock-in]] — structural dependency with no viable exit — is the consequence. [[public/society_power/technofeudalism_public|Technofeudalism]] is the political-economic structure: the state becomes a tenant on private infrastructure, subject to the landlord's terms.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Sovereignty as a Service as a structural condition, you see that the state's ability to govern depends on private commercial relationships that can be altered unilaterally by the vendor.
