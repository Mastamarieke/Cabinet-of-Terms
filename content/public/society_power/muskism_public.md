# Muskism

This is not about one man. It is about what one man's consolidation of infrastructure, media, and political access reveals about the system that enabled it.

**Literal meaning**
*Musk* — Elon Musk, CEO of Tesla and SpaceX, owner of X, founder of xAI and Neuralink. *-ism* — a system of ideas. Muskism describes the governance ideology embodied by Musk's consolidation of power.

**Origin**
Quinn Slobodian and Ben Tarnoff developed the concept in 2026 to describe the political-economic model that Musk's actions exemplify: the fusion of private infrastructure, political access, information control, and venture capital into a coherent governance programme.

> Muskism is the governance ideology that uses private infrastructure, information control, and political access as instruments of power — exemplified by but not limited to Elon Musk.

**The Appeal**
The Muskism framework has appeal as critique: it names a pattern otherwise described as individual eccentricity, making the political programme visible.

**The Friction**
[[public/society_power/technofeudalism_public|Technofeudalism]] is the economic structure; Muskism is the political programme built on it. [[public/society_power/founder_mode_public|Founder Mode]] is the organisational logic. The term risks personalising what is a structural phenomenon: the conditions for Muskism predate Musk.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Muskism as a governance model rather than a personality, you see that the question is not what Musk will do next — it is what structures made his consolidation possible and what prevents the next iteration.
