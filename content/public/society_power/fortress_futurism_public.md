# Fortress Futurism

The wall is not against the past. It is for the future — a future for some, maintained by excluding others.

**Literal meaning**
*Fortress* — a defensive structure protecting insiders from outsiders. *Futurism* — orientation toward the future as a programme. Fortress Futurism combines technological utopianism with defensive exclusion.

**Origin**
Douglas Rushkoff documented the concept in *Survival of the Richest* (2022), describing conversations with Silicon Valley billionaires planning for civilisational collapse. The term's connection to South African origins — Musk grew up in apartheid South Africa — has been explored by journalists documenting the influence of a specific experience of fortified minority privilege on Silicon Valley ideology.

> Fortress Futurism is the ideology that combines technological progress with defensive exclusion — building futures for a protected few while treating the many as a threat to be managed.

**The Appeal**
Concern about civilisational risk is not irrational. Planning for disruption is responsible. The futurism component is genuine.

**The Friction**
[[public/society_power/muskism_public|Muskism]] exemplifies the programme: infrastructure ownership as protection, information control as security, political access as gate-keeping. The fortress component is the key: who is inside, who is outside, and who decides.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see Fortress Futurism as an ideology rather than risk management, you see that the futures being built are not for everyone — and that the exclusion is a feature, not a limitation.
