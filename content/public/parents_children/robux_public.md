# Robux

The child does not spend money. They spend Robux. The money was already spent.

**Literal meaning:** Robux is the virtual currency used across the Roblox platform. Players purchase Robux with real money and spend it on in-game items, avatar customisation, game passes, and access to premium content within user-created games. The exchange rate fluctuates but is designed to be non-intuitive: the relationship between Robux and real money is deliberately obscured by the conversion step.

**Origin:** Robux launched with the Roblox platform and has been its primary monetisation mechanism since the platform's commercial scaling in the late 2000s. The virtual currency model — converting real money into platform-specific tokens — was established by earlier gaming platforms and online worlds, but Roblox deployed it at scale with a primarily child audience at a time when regulatory frameworks for in-game purchases targeting children were absent or undeveloped.

> A virtual currency that converts real money into platform tokens for a primarily child audience — using the conversion step to separate spending decisions from their financial reality.

**The Appeal:** Virtual currencies have genuine design logic. They allow micro-transactions that would be awkward in real currency, they simplify the experience of paying within a game, and they enable children to spend within set limits — parents can purchase a fixed amount of Robux and know the child cannot exceed it. For parents who want to give children some digital spending autonomy, a virtual currency can feel like a sensible tool.

**The Friction:** The conversion step is the mechanism. Once money becomes Robux, the psychological relationship to spending changes. The child is no longer spending £5 — they are spending 400 Robux, which feels like a different kind of resource. [[Predatory Design]] — platform design that maximises spending — exploits this deliberately: prices are set in Robux amounts that do not map cleanly to round currency figures, making mental arithmetic difficult. [[Loot Boxes]] — chance-based purchases available in Roblox games — add a gambling layer on top of the currency system: the child spends Robux for a random outcome, further separating the spending from its value. [[Gamification Creep]] — game logic entering non-game contexts — describes what happens when monetary exchange is made to feel like play. The child learns a relationship to money and spending that serves the platform's interests before they have the cognitive development to evaluate it.

**Why This Matters:** Robux is a case study in how financial abstraction shapes behaviour. The currency is not a neutral convenience — it is a design choice that systematically makes spending feel smaller than it is, in a context where the primary spenders are children.
