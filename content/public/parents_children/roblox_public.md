# Roblox

It looks like a game. It is also a currency system, a social network, a labour market, and — documented repeatedly — a space where adults access children.

**Literal meaning:** Roblox is a platform — not a single game — on which users can play games created by other users, socialise, and create their own experiences using the platform's development tools. It operates on a virtual currency called Robux, which can be purchased with real money and used across games on the platform. As of the mid-2020s, Roblox had over 70 million daily active users, the majority of them under sixteen.

**Origin:** Roblox was founded in 2004 by David Baszucki and Erik Cassel and launched publicly in 2006. It grew slowly before accelerating dramatically during the COVID-19 pandemic, when children's physical social lives were curtailed and digital ones expanded. The platform became one of the primary social environments for children aged six to sixteen in many countries, functioning not just as a gaming platform but as the place where many children's friendships and social lives were located.

> A platform that functions simultaneously as game, social network, economy, and labour market for children — under a single brand name that signals none of this to parents or regulators.

**The Appeal:** Roblox is genuinely engaging and, for many children, genuinely social. It enables creativity — children and teenagers build games played by millions — and provides a space for friendship and play. The user-generated model means the range of content is enormous, and much of it is age-appropriate and positively reviewed by children and parents. For many families, Roblox is where their child's social life actually happens.

**The Friction:** The platform's architecture contains several distinct risks that its name does not signal. [[Robux]] — the platform's virtual currency — introduces financial logic into children's play: real money funds virtual currency that funds in-game advantages, creating spending pressure on children and parents. [[Loot Boxes]] — chance-based purchases — embed gambling mechanics in games that children encounter without age verification. [[Online Grooming]] — the use of digital platforms to establish inappropriate contact with minors — is documented repeatedly in Roblox contexts: the platform's social features, including private messaging and in-game interaction, have been used by adults seeking access to children. [[Predatory Design]] — design maximising engagement and spending — operates throughout: notifications, reward schedules, and social features are engineered to maintain time-on-platform.

**Why This Matters:** Roblox is a case study in how a platform name functions as a shield. "My child plays Roblox" sounds like a statement about a game. It is actually a statement about participation in a virtual economy, a social network, and a space with documented safeguarding risks — none of which the name communicates.
