# Deskilling

You used to be able to do this. Now you reach for the tool before you try.

**Literal meaning**
*De-* — removal, reversal. *Skilling* — the possession of skills. Deskilling is the process by which skills atrophy through disuse, typically as automation takes over tasks.

**Origin**
Harry Braverman documented deskilling in *Labor and Monopoly Capital* (1974) — how industrial automation removed craft knowledge from workers. The concept is now applied to knowledge work as AI tools take over cognitive tasks.

> Deskilling is the gradual loss of capacity through disuse — when tools do the work, the ability to do it without tools fades.

**The Appeal**
Delegating tedious or repetitive tasks to tools is rational. AI tools handle tasks faster, more accurately, and at scale. The efficiency gain is real.

**The Friction**
[[public/ai_specific/ai_dependency_public|AI Dependency]] — structural reliance on AI without critical reflection — accumulates quietly. A student who never learns to draft without autocomplete, a professional who never analyses without AI assistance, an organisation that cannot function when the tool fails. The capacity is not lost suddenly; it fades through disuse.

**Why This Matters**
Once you see deskilling as structural rather than personal, you stop asking "am I lazy for using this?" and start asking "what do I lose when this is unavailable — and does that matter?" Sometimes it does not. Sometimes it determines whether you can think independently at all.
